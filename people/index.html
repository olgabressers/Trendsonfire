Key People & Transformation Trends — 2026
Trend observations are editorial analysis based on TrendsOnFire research and publicly available industry sources. Specific percentages without a named source are editorial estimates and should be treated as directional.
AI & Workforce
AI adoption in commerce is a people challenge, not a technology challenge
The majority of AI implementation setbacks in retail trace back to change management gaps, not technical problems. Organisations that invest in upskilling before deploying AI consistently see faster adoption and lower resistance.
Talent
The war for digital commerce talent is intensifying — and traditional retailers are losing
Digital-native brands and tech companies are outcompeting traditional retailers on talent. The differentiator is not salary — it is purpose, flexibility, and career development. Retailers that have cracked this are building elite teams.
Salary Gaps
Executive compensation gaps across Europe are wider than most leaders realise
Transformation director pay varies by up to 3× across European markets — from CHF 180–260k in Switzerland to €35–65k in Romania/Serbia (TrendsOnFire Salary Index). This gap is driving senior talent westward from CEE.
Layoffs
The Dutch director-level job market may be the tightest in a decade
Corporate restructuring across NL has created a senior talent surplus at director level and above. Based on TrendsOnFire layoff tracking, the supply of experienced transformation leaders now significantly exceeds demand in the Netherlands.
Governance
Programme governance is the unglamorous differentiator between success and drift
Organisations with strong PMO discipline — clear ownership, milestone cadence, escalation paths — consistently deliver transformations closer to their original targets than those without structured governance.
Wellbeing
Sleep deprivation is the hidden productivity crisis costing European businesses billions
A RAND Europe study estimates sleep deprivation costs the Netherlands alone €2.3B annually in lost productivity — equivalent to 1.3% of GDP (RAND, “Why Sleep Matters”). Forward-thinking HR leaders are treating sleep as a strategic people metric.
