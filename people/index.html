Key People & Transformation Trends — 2026
🏗️ Organisation Design
Flat, agile structures are replacing traditional retail hierarchies — faster than expected
Retailers redesigning their org structures around customer journeys rather than functions are seeing 30% faster decision-making and significantly higher employee engagement scores.
🤖 AI & Workforce
AI adoption in commerce is a people challenge, not a technology challenge
70% of AI implementation failures in retail trace back to change management gaps, not technical problems. Organisations that invest in upskilling before deploying AI see 3× faster adoption rates.
🌍 Culture
Culture eats transformation strategy for breakfast — every single time
Commerce companies that invest in culture alignment before launching transformation programmes see 40% lower attrition and significantly better NPS scores during change periods.
📊 Performance
OKRs are replacing KPI cascades in forward-looking commerce organisations
The shift from top-down KPI management to OKR-based goal setting is accelerating. Organisations using OKRs report 25% higher alignment between team-level activity and strategic priorities.
🧲 Talent
The war for digital commerce talent is intensifying — and traditional retailers are losing
Digital-native brands and tech companies are outcompeting traditional retailers on talent. The answer isn't salary — it's purpose, flexibility, and career development. Retailers that have cracked this are building elite teams.
📐 Governance
Programme governance is the unglamorous differentiator between success and drift
Organisations with strong PMO discipline — clear ownership, milestone cadence, escalation paths — deliver transformations 40% more likely to hit their original targets versus those without structured governance.
😴 Workforce Wellbeing
Sleep deprivation is the hidden productivity crisis costing European businesses billions
A RAND Europe study estimates sleep deprivation costs the Netherlands alone €2.3B annually in lost productivity — equivalent to 1.3% of GDP. Chronically sleep-deprived employees make more errors, resist change, and disengage faster during transformation programmes. Forward-thinking HR leaders are now treating sleep as a strategic people metric, not a personal lifestyle choice.
💶 Salary Benchmarks
Executive salary gaps across Europe are wider than most leaders realise — and they're reshaping talent flows
Transformation director compensation varies by up to 3× across European markets. Switzerland and UK lead (CHF 180–260k / £120–180k), followed by Netherlands and Germany (€110–160k), France and Belgium (€90–130k), Nordics (€100–150k), then CEE (PL/CZ/HU: €50–90k, RO/RS: €35–65k). Russia pre-2022 sat at €70–110k. This gap is driving senior talent westward from CEE while creating acute shortages in transformation leadership in Eastern markets — exactly where demand is growing fastest.
