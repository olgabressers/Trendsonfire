Netherlands Labour Market · 2023–2025

The Hidden Jobs Crisis:
Senior Talent Glut in the Netherlands

Big corporates — Nike, Booking.com, Philips, ING, Shell and more — have collectively shed tens of thousands of senior roles. The headline unemployment rate doesn't show it. This dashboard does.

Scope: Netherlands · Director-level and above Sources: company announcements, CBS, LinkedIn data Updated 14 April 2026

The Netherlands unemployment rate has risen to 4.1% in February 2026 — the highest level since September 2021 (CBS). For 15–24 year-olds the figure has reached 9.1%. But the headline number is structurally blind to the senior crisis: a wave of highly-paid senior professionals, made redundant by large multinationals restructuring for an AI-first world, is now competing for a shrinking pool of equivalent roles. What looks like a healthy labour market is quietly a brutal market at the top.

Key Insights
The Director Trap
Directors are the most displaced — and the hardest to place
Director-level roles are disproportionately affected by corporate restructuring. Companies hiring today are skipping this layer — pushing work to VPs or promoting seniors directly. The market has structurally depressed demand for this tier.
The Big Corp Wave
Nike, Booking, Philips — the names that changed the NL job market
These are not failing companies. They are profitable businesses cutting senior costs to fund AI investment and satisfy shareholders. The result: talented, experienced people flooded the market simultaneously — with nearly identical CVs.
The Hidden Market
Most senior roles are never posted publicly
With hundreds of applications per posted senior role, companies are actively avoiding job boards. Interim roles, executive search, and warm referrals dominate. Candidates applying to posted vacancies are competing in the most crowded segment of the market.
Interim as an Escape Valve
Interim & consulting is absorbing displaced senior talent
Companies still need expertise — but are reluctant to add permanent senior cost. Interim, fractional, and project-based engagements are absorbing some of the supply. Senior professionals who pivot to interim are finding routes back to market faster.
AI Acceleration
AI is the stated rationale — but the cuts predate real AI ROI
Many companies cited "AI efficiency gains" to justify cuts in 2023–2024, before those gains materialised. The honest driver is margin expansion: senior salaries (€120k–€200k+) are the fastest lever. AI provides political cover for what is fundamentally a cost restructure.
Market Outlook
2026 may see partial recovery — but the structural layer is permanent
Hiring signals from scale-ups and mid-market companies suggest some absorption of senior talent in 2026. But the flattened org model — fewer directors, more direct VP spans — is structural. The pre-2022 layer of Dutch corporate management will not fully return.
