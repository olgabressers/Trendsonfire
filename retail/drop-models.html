Mass Market — Nike · New Balance
Drops at scale: millions of entries, hundreds of thousands of users, and a 93% unfulfillment rate

Nike's SNKRS platform attracted $1.69 billion in total consumer demand across 2025 — and met 7% of it. Drop attendance has more than doubled from a historical average of 300,000 users to 750,000 for top releases. The Air Jordan 11 "Gamma" alone, released December 13, generated 19,079 trades on StockX within days — the second-largest single-release trading day in StockX history. New Balance ran a parallel strategy with collaboration drops, and ended the year at $9.2 billion in global sales — up 19%.

$9.2 billion. Five consecutive years of double-digit growth. Collab drops as the primary demand engine.

Affordable Luxury — Moncler Genius · Jacquemus · Adidas
Where cultural spectacle meets the drop: 825 million interactions, site crashes, and the Samba effect

The middle tier of the drop model runs on cultural events as much as product scarcity. Moncler Genius staged its largest global event in Shanghai in October 2025, reaching 8.9 billion potential audience members and 63 million livestream viewers — then released the collections into market. Jacquemus turned a collaboration with Nike into a site crash and 500,000 Instagram interactions. Adidas closed the year with its Samba Jane as the platform's #1 new model on StockX.

Moncler Genius — The City of Genius, Shanghai
10 designers. One city. Collections released to market the same week.

Moncler Genius staged its largest event ever in Shanghai in October 2025, presenting 10 collections created in collaboration with Rick Owens, A$AP Rocky, Palm Angels, Jil Sander, Willow Smith, Donald Glover, Mercedes-Benz by Nigo, Edward Enninful, Hiroshi Fujiwara, and Lulu Li. The event reached a potential global audience of 8.9 billion people in the first week.

The Genius model — one brand, multiple creative directors, regular limited-edition drops — is Moncler's primary demand-creation engine. It allows the brand to generate fresh cultural relevance without a single creative-director signature, and to build a calendar of drops that sustain secondary market activity year-round.

Moncler Group closed FY2025 with €3.13 billion in consolidated revenues, up 3% at constant currency. The Q4 2025 quarter grew 7% at constant currency, driven in part by strong Asian market response to the Shanghai Genius event.

Luxury — Supreme · Louis Vuitton · Hermès · Chanel
17 seconds. 130% resale. 138% value retention. What the data shows when scarcity is structural.

At the top of the market, the drop model operates on different mechanics. Supreme tracks its own sellout times to the second: its FW25 b.b. Simon fleece jacket sold out in 17 seconds. Louis Vuitton ran three Murakami collaboration chapters in the first half of 2025, with styles exceeding 130% resale value and generating six times the search interest on the Rebag platform. Hermès achieved average value retention of 138% in 2025. Chanel raised prices 4–7% and saw secondary market activity described as "at record levels" by Sotheby's.

Hermès — Scarcity as System
12,000–15,000 Birkins produced per year. Zero formal waitlist. Access through relationship.

Hermès does not operate drops in the traditional sense. Birkin and Kelly bag production is estimated at 12,000–15,000 units per year globally — a fraction of worldwide demand. Formal waitlists were discontinued; the brand operates through a relationship-based allocation system where access correlates with purchase history across the full Hermès catalogue.

The Rebag Clair Report (December 2025) placed Hermès average value retention at 138% for the year — up 38 percentage points from 2024. The Kelly Mini II reached 282% of original retail. Sotheby's reported a 44% increase in Hermès bag trading volume year on year, with average sale prices up 35%. A single Jane Birkin prototype sold at Sotheby's Paris in July 2025 for $10.1 million.

In a year when the broader luxury market contracted — LVMH Fashion & Leather declined 12% in Q2 2025, Kering fell 16% — Hermès leather goods grew 12%, confirming that structural scarcity outperforms manufactured urgency in a softening market.

Market Context — Resale · Consumer Behaviour · Luxury Market
47% of drops profitable on resale in 2025, down from 58% in 2020. The bifurcation is structural.

The resale market for limited editions reached $10.6 billion in global GMV in 2025. But the share of drops that actually trade above retail continued to compress — from 58% in 2020 to 47% in 2025. At the same time, ultra-limited collaborations still command extreme premiums: Travis Scott x Jordan averaged $451 on StockX, a 197% markup. The market is not crashing; it is bifurcating between mass scarcity (which no longer works consistently) and genuine scarcity (which continues to outperform).

The structural read: The drop model bifurcated in 2025. For mass-market releases — Nike Dunks, Air Jordan 1s available at scale — resale premiums collapsed to 10–25%. For ultra-limited collaborations (Tom Sachs, Fragment × Travis Scott, Bad Bunny × Adidas) premiums remained at 3–12× retail. At the luxury end, Hermès and Louis Vuitton × Murakami demonstrated that structural scarcity — where supply is permanently below demand — generates value retention that pure drop mechanics cannot replicate. The 47% of drops now trading below retail do not signal the death of the model; they signal that the model has normalized and that the 53% without premiums were never truly scarce to begin with.
