Where Sporting Goods Brands Are Losing — and Where the White Space Is

A competitive gap analysis across 10 major brands: product coverage, purchase channels, loyalty, sustainability and emerging consumer opportunities. Updated Q1 2026.

Updated Q1 2026 Scope: Global + Europe Sources: Company reports, Euromonitor, consumer surveys
Consumer spending on non-essential sporting goods dropped −8% in Europe in 2024 as cost-of-living pressures squeezed discretionary budgets. At the same time, Nike and Adidas lost combined market share for the second consecutive year — not to each other, but to value brands and challenger brands that filled specific unmet needs. The opportunity is real: most large sporting goods brands are still competing on the same axes — logo, athlete sponsorship, and hero product drops. The brands that win the next cycle will compete on services, community, and circular value.

Methodology & Sources

This analysis draws on publicly available data from company annual reports and investor presentations (2024/2025), Euromonitor International sportswear market data, consumer sentiment surveys conducted across 6 European markets (n=4,200), Google Trends data for search interest indexing, and desk research into brand capability mapping.

  • Gap matrix ratings reflect analyst assessment based on publicly observable brand capabilities as of Q1 2026.
  • DTC revenue figures are estimates based on disclosed data and analyst consensus where not publicly stated.
  • Consumer value-for-money scores are survey-derived indices, not official brand metrics.
  • White space opportunities represent strategic hypotheses for exploration, not guaranteed market positions.
