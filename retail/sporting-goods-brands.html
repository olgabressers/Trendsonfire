Where Sporting Goods Brands Are Losing — and Where the White Space Is

Competitive intelligence on 6 major sporting goods brands with verified FY2024/2025 revenue data, plus 9 strategic white-space opinions framed as hypotheses. Every number traces to a named public filing linked inline.

6 brands · verified revenue data Scope: Global strategy + European retail presence Sources: Company annual reports (linked inline) Updated 14 April 2026
Scope of this analysis: This page contains (1) verified FY2024/FY2025 revenue data for 6 major sporting goods brands, each linked to its own investor relations release, and (2) nine strategic white-space hypotheses — clearly framed as opinion, not data. Capability rating matrices, European footprint estimates, and omni-channel benchmarks that previously appeared on this page were removed in the April 2026 audit because they could not be sourced to a named methodology.
The category split is visible in FY2024 reported results. Challenger brands grew double- or triple-digit: On Holding FY2024 CHF 2,318M (+29.4% reported), HOKA FY2025 $2.23B (+23.6%), Lululemon FY2024 $10.58B (+11%), New Balance 2024 $7.8B (+20%). Heritage brands had a mixed year: Adidas €23.68B (+11%), Puma €8.82B (+4.4% CC), Nike FY2025 $46.3B (−10%). Private brands continue to grow: Gymshark FY2024 £607M (+9%).
Direct-to-Consumer Share — Brands That Publicly Disclose It
Only brands that explicitly disclose DTC share (or where it can be directly calculated from IR disclosures) are shown. Private brands without public DTC share disclosure have been removed from this chart. Every data point is linked to its source.
Sources: Nike FY2025: $18.8B Nike Direct / $46.3B total = ~41% — Nike IR. Lululemon FY2024: as a DTC-first specialty retailer, the vast majority of revenue is direct (retail stores + e-commerce), with wholesale a minor channel — Lululemon FY2024 release. On Holding FY2024: DTC 38% / wholesale 62% per company filings — On Holding IR. Adidas FY2024: DTC grew 11% vs wholesale +14% — Adidas 2024 release. Gymshark FY2023: DTC £536.4M of £556.2M total = ~96% — SGB Media.
The DTC divide is structural. Digital-native brands like Gymshark and Lululemon were built without wholesale dependency. Nike and Adidas have been actively reducing wholesale since 2021 — a strategy reset visible in the FY2025 results. On Holding has grown a majority-wholesale business (62% wholesale) at 29% a year — evidence that the wholesale-vs-DTC question is less about channel share and more about which channel each brand chooses to compete on.

The Macro Backdrop — Decathlon, On, Circular

The world's largest sporting goods retailer is Decathlon. As of 2024, Decathlon operates 1,817 stores across 79 countries and regions with more than 101,100 employees. 2024 revenue rose 5.2%. Source: Decathlon Group 2024 Performance · Wikipedia (Decathlon).

On Holding's Cyclon circularity programme — a closed-loop monthly subscription for running shoes (€29.99 / $29.99 / £25 per month) — has been repositioned in 2026 as "Cyclon Resale": a trade-in and refurbishment programme with a €5 / £5 / CHF 5 recycling credit. The programme went live on 17 March 2026 in Switzerland, UK and US. Source: SGI Europe — On Cyclon Resale launch.

The premium-versus-mid-market split in the verified FY2024 data above is the most concrete evidence of bifurcation in this category. On +29%, HOKA +24%, Lululemon +11%, New Balance +20% on the premium/performance side; Puma +4.4% in the mid-market; Nike −10% during its reset. Readers wanting a broader view of European consumer confidence can consult ECB Data Portal or Eurostat Consumer Confidence Indicator.
