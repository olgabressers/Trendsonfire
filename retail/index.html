Key Retail & E-Commerce Trends — 2026
Trend observations are editorial analysis based on the TrendsOnFire NL Inventory Visibility Monitor (43 retailers, March 2026) and publicly available industry research. Specific percentages without a named source are editorial estimates and should be treated as directional.
Inventory Visibility
Real-time stock transparency is now a conversion driver, not a nice-to-have
Retailers showing live stock levels, low-stock alerts, and variant availability are likely to see materially higher add-to-cart rates. Yet based on the TrendsOnFire NL audit, nearly half of NL fashion retailers still do not surface stock signals at variant level.
Fulfilment
Delivery date accuracy has become a trust signal — not just a logistics metric
Shoppers treat promised delivery dates as a purchase commitment. Based on the TrendsOnFire NL audit, only about half of NL retailers currently show delivery dates tied to real inventory — the rest show generic estimates or no date at all.
Store Integration
Store-level inventory visibility is the new baseline for omni-channel retail
Department stores and electronics retailers lead on store-level stock visibility. Fashion lags significantly — only 4 out of 13 NL fashion brands assessed in the TrendsOnFire monitor allow shoppers to check store-level availability online.
Engagement
Back-in-stock notifications: the most under-used re-engagement tool in retail
Back-in-stock alerts are a proven demand recovery mechanism. Based on the TrendsOnFire NL audit, luxury brands are missing this almost entirely — only 2 of 5 NL luxury brands assessed offer this feature.
Luxury Retail
Luxury’s deliberate scarcity strategy conflicts with digital shopper expectations
Luxury brands resist low-stock urgency messaging to protect brand positioning. But digital-native luxury shoppers increasingly expect transparency. In the TrendsOnFire NL audit, Tiffany & Co leads with 5/6 criteria met.
Checkout Experience
Simplified checkout is no longer a luxury — it is an expectation
BNPL, digital wallets, and one-click options are now baseline expectations for shoppers under 45. Brands that have not simplified payment flows may be losing a significant share of carts at checkout — an industry-wide pattern consistently reported by Baymard Institute research.
BOPIS
Buy Online, Pick Up In Store is no longer differentiating — it is table stakes
Click & collect adoption has crossed the tipping point across European retail. The new battleground is speed: same-hour pickup is emerging as the next frontier for retailers with dense store networks.
B2B Marketplaces
Vertical B2B marketplaces are the largest structural opportunity in European tech
European B2B e-commerce may exceed €1.5 trillion, yet fewer than 12% of B2B transactions happen through a dedicated marketplace — compared to 35%+ in B2C (Euromonitor, Dealroom estimates). Vertical specialists who own the workflow in one sector are winning over horizontal platforms.
Sporting Goods
The DTC divide in sporting goods is structural — and widening
Brands born DTC (Gymshark, Lululemon) have no wholesale dependency to unwind. Heritage brands below 25% DTC remain reliant on partners who control pricing and data. Nike’s FY2025 revenue fell 10% to $46.3B (Nike IR) as the DTC-first strategy underperformed in EMEA.
Energy Flexibility
Europe’s electricity grid is shifting from central generation to distributed flexibility
Batteries, industrial loads, EVs, and heat pumps are bidding into balancing markets in real time. FCR prices reached €400–600/MW/week in NL and BE during tight periods in 2024–2025 (ENTSO-E, national TSO publications). Aggregators and platform operators who can onboard distributed assets at scale are the emerging winners.
Online Furniture
Pure-play online furniture keeps failing in Europe — the unit economics do not work
Fonq collapsed after 18 years. Return rates of 15–30% on bulky items, last-mile delivery costs, and housing market sensitivity make the pure-play model structurally unprofitable in most European markets. Omni-channel players with showrooms are winning.
