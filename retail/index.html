Trend Intelligence

Checkout Experience
The average documented e-commerce cart abandonment rate sits close to 70%
Baymard Institute aggregates 49 studies and reports an average documented cart abandonment rate of 70.19%. Baymard's own checkout UX benchmark attributes a large share to preventable friction — mandatory account creation, complicated checkout, limited payment options. Simplified checkout (BNPL, wallets, one-click) addresses the top drivers. Source: Baymard Institute
B2B Marketplaces
Vertical B2B marketplaces remain a large structural opportunity in European tech
B2B e-commerce penetration is materially behind B2C in most European sectors. Vertical specialists that own the workflow in one sector are positioned to win over horizontal platforms. For current funding, deal data and named acquirers, see Dealroom. Source: Dealroom — European B2B marketplaces
Sporting Goods
Nike FY2025 revenue fell ~10% to $46.3B as the DTC-first strategy underperformed in EMEA
Nike's FY2025 financial filings reported ~$46.3B revenue, down about 10% year-on-year. Heritage sporting-goods brands below 25% DTC remain reliant on wholesale partners who control pricing and data, while DTC-native brands have no wholesale relationships to unwind. Source: Nike Investor Relations
Energy Flexibility
Europe's electricity grid is shifting from central generation to distributed flexibility
Batteries, industrial loads, EVs, and heat pumps are bidding into balancing markets in real time. In the Netherlands, BEVs reached 40.2% of new car sales in early 2025. Belgium's CRM Y-1 auction for 2025-2026 cleared at ~€183M. Aggregators that can onboard distributed assets at scale are emerging winners. Sources: electrive, Elia
Online Furniture
Pure-play online furniture keeps failing in Europe — the unit economics do not work
Fonq filed for bankruptcy on 27 March 2026, 23 years after founding. Furniture online return rates averaged ~17% in 2024, and processing a single furniture return can cost a meaningful share of the item's original price. Omni-channel players with showrooms are winning. Sources: DutchNews, upCounting
Digital Services
EU Digital Services Act (DSA) obligations apply to every online marketplace serving EU users
The Digital Services Act (Regulation EU 2022/2065), in full application since 17 February 2024, requires marketplaces to verify trader identity, act on illegal content notices, and disclose recommender-system parameters. Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs, 45M+ EU users) carry additional systemic-risk obligations. Source: European Commission — DSA
