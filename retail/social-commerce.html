The Social Commerce Race

Ten years of every major platform betting on checkout — who won, who retreated, and who is still waiting to decide. Platform-by-platform data from 2015 to 2026.

Updated 2 May 2026 Global social commerce: $699B in 2024 TikTok Shop US: $15.1B in year two Meta deprecated checkout September 2025
01
The Decade
From Buyable Pins to a $699B market — in ten years
Social commerce did not exist as a transaction category in 2014. By 2024, it had become a $699B global market. The decade was not a straight line — it was a series of platform bets, retreats, and one latecomer that grew faster than anything before it.

Platform Launch Timeline — 2015 to 2026
When each platform introduced, expanded, or abandoned commerce features
Jun 2015
Pinterest launches Buyable Pins — the first major social checkout
Direct in-app purchase for products pinned from US retailers. The first attempt by any major social platform to close the loop between discovery and transaction. · TechCrunch, June 2015
2016–2017
Instagram launches product tagging and shoppable posts
Product tags in feed posts, then Stories shopping stickers in 2018. Discovery-first model — tapping a tag links to the brand's external site. No checkout on-platform yet.
Oct 2018
Pinterest retires Buyable Pins — replaces with Product Pins
Three years after launching the first social checkout, Pinterest steps back. Buyable Pins are replaced by Product Pins, which link out to merchant sites rather than closing the sale in-app. · Clix Marketing, Oct 2018
Mar 2019
Instagram launches native in-app checkout — 26 launch brands
Full closed-loop commerce: browse → purchase without leaving the app. Launch partners include Nike, Zara, Warby Parker, Revolve. The first major Western platform to offer this since Pinterest's 2015 attempt. · TechCrunch
May 2020
Facebook Shops launches for SMB storefronts
Facebook and Instagram Shops introduced as a unified commerce layer, positioned as a tool for small businesses to build in-app storefronts. Checkout available for US merchants on both platforms. · GoDataFeed
2020–2021
TikTok Shop launches in UK and Southeast Asia
TikTok Shop goes live in the UK and across Southeast Asian markets, combining short-video discovery with integrated checkout and affiliate creator commissions in a single product.
Jul 2022
YouTube Shopping launches with Shopify integration
YouTube introduces product tagging in videos, livestream shopping, and a dedicated shopping explore destination. Shopify integration enables direct checkout links. Available initially in US, India, Brazil. · TechCrunch, July 2022
Sep 2023
TikTok Shop launches in the United States
US market entry after two years of Southeast Asian and UK operation. Reaches $9B GMV in its first full year (2024) — the fastest GMV ramp ever recorded for a social commerce platform. · Momentum Works, Jan 2025
Sep 2025
Meta deprecates native checkout on Facebook and Instagram
Six years after launching in-app checkout, Meta reverses course. Facebook and Instagram Shops now redirect users to external merchant websites rather than completing purchases in-app. TikTok Shop is left as the only platform in Western markets maintaining closed-loop commerce. · GoDataFeed · Meta Business Help Center
Mar 2026
Instagram relaunches creator affiliate commerce
After shutting down its native affiliate tool in August 2022, Instagram re-enters creator affiliate commerce — years after TikTok Shop affiliates had already become a $15B+ business in the US alone. · Net Influencer, 2026
Sources: TechCrunch platform announcement archives · GoDataFeed — Meta checkout deprecation · Momentum Works, Jan 2025

The Winner
TikTok Shop — the fastest GMV ramp in platform history
TikTok Shop launched in the US in September 2023 with no US track record and entered a market where Meta had been running native checkout for four years. By year two it had $15.1B in US GMV alone — and was accelerating.

03
The Strategic Split
Four platforms, four different answers to the same question
Every major platform has faced the same decision: build the checkout, or keep the discovery. In 2026, the answers have diverged — and the consequences are visible in the data.
TikTok Shop
Closed-loop commerce
18.2%
US social commerce share, 2025
The only platform in Western markets with a fully integrated closed-loop checkout after Meta's September 2025 retreat. Combines short-video discovery, livestream selling, and affiliate creator commissions in a single product. 60% of GMV is creator-driven — the commerce and the content are inseparable.
$15.1B US GMV in 2025 · projected 24.1% US share by 2027 · eMarketer, 2025
Meta (Instagram + Facebook)
Checkout deprecated 2025
Sep '25
Native checkout retired after 6 years
Launched in-app checkout on Instagram in March 2019 and Facebook Shops in May 2020. By September 2025, both were deprecated — Shops now redirect to external merchant sites. Instagram re-entered creator affiliate commerce in March 2026, but without the closed-loop checkout that TikTok Shop has maintained throughout.
Discovery and redirect model only. 44% of Instagram users shop on the platform weekly — but the transaction completes on the merchant's own site. · GoDataFeed
Pinterest
Highest intent, no checkout
85%
Of weekly users made a purchase from a Pin
Pinterest has the highest documented purchase intent of any social platform — yet has not maintained a native checkout since retiring Buyable Pins in 2018. 553 million monthly active users as of Q4 2024 (+11% YoY). Pinners spend 2× as much monthly as shoppers on other social platforms. Every transaction completes on an external merchant site.
32% higher ROAS than other digital platforms (Nielsen / Pinterest). Full-year 2024 revenue: $3.6B (+19% YoY) — all advertising, no commerce GMV disclosed. · Pinterest Q4 2024 · Sprout Social / Morning Consult
YouTube Shopping
Building — no GMV disclosed
5×
GMV growth year-on-year, 2025
Launched in July 2022 with Shopify integration. By July 2025, 500,000+ creators enrolled in YouTube Shopping globally. At the September 2025 Made on YouTube event, YouTube announced 5× year-on-year GMV growth — without disclosing an absolute dollar figure. 52% of users turn to YouTube for product research before buying.
No absolute GMV figure has been published. Growing fast from a small base. · TechCrunch, Sep 2025 · SellersCommerce, Apr 2026

The structural split, in one sentence: in 2026, one Western platform has closed-loop social commerce (TikTok Shop), two platforms route discovery to external checkout (Pinterest, Instagram), and one is building toward a number it has not yet disclosed (YouTube).

The market data is clear about where the growth has gone. What is not yet clear is whether TikTok Shop's structural advantage — creator-driven closed-loop GMV — is a durable moat or a first-mover position that other platforms can replicate once they decide to commit.
