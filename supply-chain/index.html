Key Supply Chain Trends — 2026
🔄 Device Lifecycle
Circular economy regulation is forcing telcos to build reverse logistics at scale
EU WEEE Directive tightening and Right to Repair legislation are pushing Vodafone, KPN, and T-Mobile to invest heavily in device return, refurbishment, and resale. DLM is shifting from cost centre to revenue contributor.
🏭 3PL Strategy
Logistics outsourcing contracts are growing in complexity — and risk
3PL contracts now span device ownership, financing, refurbishment SLAs, and data destruction compliance. Contract managers need operational depth — not just procurement skills — to manage these partnerships effectively.
📊 Supply Chain Data
End-to-end supply chain visibility is now a competitive differentiator
Real-time tracking from device dispatch through return, triage, refurbishment, and resale is becoming table stakes. Operators without it are flying blind on inventory position and margin leakage.
♻️ Sustainability
Refurbished device sales are growing 3x faster than new device sales in Europe
Consumer appetite for certified-refurbished devices is surging. Telcos with strong DLM operations are capturing margin on devices they already own — turning end-of-lease returns into a secondary revenue stream.
🔗 Outsourcing
CEVA, Ingram Micro, and Foxconn are competing for telco DLM contracts
The European DLM services market is consolidating. CEVA Logistics, Ingram Micro Lifecycle, and Foxconn Industrial all offer end-to-end device processing. Differentiation is now on SLA performance and data compliance.
💰 Financing
Asset-backed device financing is creating new supply chain complexity
When devices are financed through third-party lenders (like Macquarie), the logistics operator must coordinate between carrier, lender, and customer at end-of-contract. Tripartite coordination is the hidden complexity in DLM.
