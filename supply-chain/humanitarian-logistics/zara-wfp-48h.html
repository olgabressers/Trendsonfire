Humanitarian Logistics — Brief 01

Zara restocks in 48 hours. WFP fed 124 million people. The gap is the operating environment.

Same speed discipline. Completely different operating conditions.

12 May 2026 Sources: Inditex, WFP Annual Review 2024, DHL Group

Zara restocks every store on the planet in 48 hours. DHL's Disaster Response Teams deploy into disaster zones in 72. The 24-hour gap between them is the operating environment.

Inditex runs on predictable demand, contracted carriers, pre-cleared customs, and nine distribution centres in Spain. New collections reach every European store in 36 hours. Stable infrastructure, commercial incentives, SLA enforcement at every point in the chain.

DHL's Disaster Response Teams activate within 72 hours of a UN call — into collapsed airports, flooded access routes, and areas no commercial courier reaches. 900+ trained volunteers across four regional hubs provide coverage of 80% of global disaster hotspots. No SLA. No revenue model. No predictable geography.

Behind both operations: the same disciplines. Demand sensing, pre-positioning, carrier coordination, last-mile execution.
