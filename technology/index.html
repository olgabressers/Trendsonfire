Trend Intelligence

Electric Vehicle Evolution
Netherlands becomes the first European country to approve Tesla FSD (Supervised)
On 10 April 2026, the Dutch vehicle authority RDW granted Tesla type approval for Full Self-Driving (Supervised) under UN Regulation R-171 — the first national approval in the EU. Tesla FSD operates at SAE Level 2, requiring driver supervision at all times. Germany, France, Belgium, Spain and Italy are expected to follow by summer 2026. Source: Electrek
AI in Retail
AI adoption is now mainstream and accelerating — the gap to laggards is widening
McKinsey's State of AI 2025 finds 78% of organisations now use AI in at least one business function, up from 55% in 2023. Generative AI adoption climbed to 71%. The strategic implication: organisations without an AI roadmap are now in a structural minority. Source: McKinsey — The state of AI in 2025
Operating Models
The AI-first operating model requires role redesign, not just tool deployment
McKinsey's 2025 research finds that high performers are 3.6x more likely to pursue transformational change when deploying AI, and 55% fundamentally rework workflows rather than bolting AI onto existing processes. Source: McKinsey — The state of AI in 2025
Data Strategy
First-party data is a strategic asset as third-party signals decline
Google completed third-party cookie deprecation in Chrome through 2024-2025 and Apple ATT (introduced 2021) materially reduced third-party identifier signal across iOS. Retailers and commerce businesses that invested in zero-party and first-party data are positioned for the post-cookie environment. Sources: Google Privacy Sandbox, Apple ATT
Integration
Composable / MACH architecture has moved from emerging trend to mainstream
According to MACH Alliance research, 61% of enterprise technology stacks will be MACH-based by 2026 with 8 in 10 organisations reporting positive outlook on the approach. Gartner's 2025 DXP Magic Quadrant treats composability as a mandatory requirement. Source: MACH Alliance
Digital Trust
EU AI Act introduces transparency obligations for AI systems interacting with consumers
The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) requires that users be informed when they are interacting with AI systems. Prohibited-risk AI applies from 2 February 2025; transparency and high-risk obligations roll out through 2026-2027. Brands that get ahead of disclosure obligations build consumer trust as a competitive differentiator. Sources: EU AI Act portal, EUR-Lex
