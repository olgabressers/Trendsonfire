Beerwulf was created by Heineken in 2016 as a standalone venture reporting directly to Heineken's board, with its own decision-making authority. It operates as a retailer — not a brewer — selling competitors' brands alongside Heineken's own portfolio. The platform launched to the public in March 2017 after a 9-month build and grew from 400 orders per week to 400 orders per hour within its first year.

The business is built around the BLADE home draught system: a professional-grade beer tap appliance for home use, paired with an ongoing supply of 8-litre kegs across Heineken's brand portfolio. The model combines a hardware sale with a recurring keg subscription — a hybrid physical/digital commerce model operating across 7 European markets simultaneously.

Context for the CX role

Beerwulf's customer lifecycle spans hardware purchase → keg subscription → multi-market delivery → hardware support. Each step generates distinct CX needs: onboarding (setup, first pour), subscription management, logistics queries, and hardware failure resolution. The Head of CX sits at the intersection of all of them, operating across BPO-managed customer care in multiple languages.

02
Product & Customer Model
BLADE System

Home draught hardware + recurring keg supply

8-litre kegs chilled to 2°C. Stays fresh for 30 days. Approx. 14 pints per keg. Per-pint cost from £1.98. Dimensions: 590×290×471mm. 17.6kg base weight. Standard warranty 2 years; extended warranty £49 for 3 years. Brands: Heineken, Birra Moretti, Cruzcampo, Tiger, Affligem, Edelweiss, Gösser, and others.

Subscription & Repeat Purchase

Monthly and bi-monthly keg delivery options

Subscription model available for frequent buyers — monthly or bi-monthly cadence. The recurring revenue model creates predictable fulfilment demand and a defined lifecycle: acquisition → first hardware purchase → keg subscription → retention. Subscription health is a key leading indicator for CX performance: early churn typically tied to setup friction or delivery experience.

B2B Channel

BLADE for Business — hospitality and office use

Separate B2B proposition for bars, offices, and hospitality venues. Originally a hospitality product, BLADE has a distinct B2B support and fulfilment track. B2B customers have higher keg volume, different SLA expectations, and distinct onboarding needs versus consumers.

Discontinuation Risk

The SUB: discontinued May 2024

The SUB Classic and SUB Compact were discontinued from May 2024. 2-litre SUB kegs discontinued December 2025. Generated significant customer dissatisfaction: customers who had recently purchased machines left with no keg supply. Communication of the decision was flagged by customers as insufficient. This is a live CX legacy issue the incoming Head of CX will inherit.

Marketplace — Mirakl Platform

Third-party marketplace launched November 2022

Beerwulf expanded from a direct retailer to an open marketplace using Mirakl, adding 80+ independent brewery sellers alongside its own direct assortment. The marketplace launch covered 10 European countries, positioning Beerwulf as "the leading B2C online beer platform in Europe." Provides a CX complexity layer: marketplace seller fulfilment introduces a second tier of customer service accountability beyond Beerwulf's own operations.

Beerwulf's Trustpilot profile shows a pattern consistent with a multi-market subscription hardware business: strong scores for product quality and delivery when things work; concentrated complaint volume around hardware issues, customer service responsiveness, and the SUB discontinuation. The 4.0 score across 51,000+ reviews indicates a structurally functional CX operation with identifiable gaps — not a broken one.

Key CX pressure points from the public review record: speed of resolution on hardware faults, clarity of communication during product changes (the SUB discontinuation), and consistency of service quality across markets. These map directly to the core scope of the Head of CX role: BPO performance, SLA management, and proactive customer communication.

What the review data signals for the incoming Head of CX

A 4.0 score on 51K reviews is a solid baseline. The gap between 4.0 and 4.4–4.5 sits in resolution speed and proactive communication. The SUB discontinuation created a customer trust deficit that is still generating complaints. The near-term CX priority is structured resolution of legacy hardware complaints and a communication playbook for future product transitions — not a full CX rebuild.

The Head of Customer Experience at Beerwulf owns five distinct accountability areas simultaneously. The role is director-level, reporting into the commercial leadership team, and acts as the Privacy Officer for all CX-related customer data — adding a GDPR compliance dimension to a commercial operations role.

The BPO management dimension is significant: Beerwulf's scale across 7 European markets means customer care is almost certainly outsourced, with the Head of CX accountable for partner performance rather than direct team management of agents.

CX Trends 2026 — Relevant to the Role

Six trends reshaping e-commerce customer experience in 2026, each with direct implications for Beerwulf's CX strategy.

  • 1

    Value-seeking customers are becoming less loyal

    Nearly half of consumers report worsening financial security. During recessionary periods, consumers prioritise certainty over brand loyalty and seek ways to stretch purchasing power. For a subscription hardware business like Beerwulf, this means subscription pause and cancellation rates are a leading CX and commercial signal — and proactive retention outreach becomes a revenue-protective function, not just a service gesture.

    ~50% report financial security worsening
  • 2

    Erosion of human support is consumers' top AI concern

    Half of consumers cite loss of human support as their top concern about AI. Nearly 1 in 5 customers report zero benefit from AI-powered support, which performs 12 points below average on usefulness. More than 4 in 5 say they are more likely to remain loyal to companies that prioritise human service. For Beerwulf's BPO model, the implication is that AI deflection without a clear human escalation path actively damages retention rather than reducing cost.

    80%+ more loyal to companies prioritising human service AI support 12 pts below average on usefulness
  • 3

    Product discovery now starts with AI chatbots

    Over one-third of consumers trust AI tools to influence their purchases. Customer journeys increasingly begin with third-party AI tools like ChatGPT before arriving on brand websites. For Beerwulf, this means the product comparison and consideration stage is happening outside the beerwulf.com environment — and the website must be structured to serve both AI crawlers and human visitors arriving pre-informed.

    35%+ trust AI for purchase decisions
  • 4

    Data privacy demands explicit value in exchange for data

    While two-thirds of consumers will share data for personalised experiences, generic discounts no longer suffice. Companies must offer quantified savings, special access, or time-saving advantages tailored to specific customer segments. For the Head of CX acting as Privacy Officer, this creates a direct link between GDPR consent management and commercial email performance: the value proposition inside the consent mechanism determines database quality.

    65% will share data for personalisation
  • 5

    Website design must serve both AI and human visitors

    As agentic AI expands, sites need optimised readability for algorithms alongside humans. Companies must ensure their brand names appear in AI summaries and design experiences for visitors arriving via AI answer engines. The practical implication for CX: FAQ content, product specs, and support documentation quality now affects AI-driven product discovery — CX content is SEO content.

  • 6

    Loyalty programmes need restrained, personalised messaging

    More than 9 in 10 consumers want customisable reward features. But customers prefer infrequent, helpful communications — like quarterly savings reports — over frequent outreach that feels intrusive. For Beerwulf's email programme, this is a frequency and relevance calibration challenge: the CX team owns a commercial email channel and must balance engagement volume against unsubscribe risk.

    90%+ want customisable loyalty features

Key CX Metrics — Industry Benchmarks

Benchmarks relevant to the Beerwulf context: subscription e-commerce, multi-market BPO, and email engagement.

Retention

5% retention increase = 95% profit increase

For a subscription hardware model, the CX function is directly accretive to margin. Every percentage point of reduced churn compounds on the keg recurring revenue stream.

Subscription Onboarding

92% renewal likelihood with effective training

Hardware products with a learning curve — like the BLADE — see significantly higher subscription renewal when the onboarding experience reduces first-use friction. 86% more loyal when businesses invest in onboarding education.

Personalisation

40% higher revenue vs non-personalised competitors

65% of consumers expect tailored experiences. For email programme ownership at Beerwulf, segmentation by market, keg usage frequency, and hardware vintage is the baseline personalisation layer.

Omnichannel

250% higher purchase rates vs single-channel

90% higher retention through omnichannel engagement. For Beerwulf, the CX touchpoints span web, email, chatbot, phone (BPO), and in-app (if applicable) — coordinating these consistently across 7 markets is the omnichannel CX challenge.

AI in CX

95% of interactions involving AI expected by 2026

80% chatbot adoption predicted for customer service. 90% of companies currently using AI for CX. The critical metric is not adoption rate but resolution rate: AI that deflects without resolving damages the 4.0 Trustpilot score.

The CX Perception Gap

8% of customers vs 80% of businesses believe superior CX is delivered

59% feel businesses have lost the human element. 54% believe most companies need CX improvement. For Beerwulf, with a 4.0 score on 51K reviews, the gap between internal perception and customer reality is a known risk — closing it requires the feedback-to-insight loop the Head of CX is responsible for building.
