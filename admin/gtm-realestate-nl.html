Why real estate agencies specifically? Every Dutch real estate agent needs local market data for three recurring jobs: (1) winning listing meetings against competitors, (2) advising buyers on price and timing, (3) maintaining a professional LinkedIn presence. They currently do this manually — pulling from NVM, Funda, CBS, and their own memory. TrendsOnFire replaces 4–6 hours of monthly research with one automated report delivered on the 12th. The ROI case is immediate and unambiguous.
Segment Analysis — Which Agency to Target First?
Recommendation

Start with boutique independents (1–5 agents). Scale to mid-size. Avoid franchises until year 2.

The boutique segment has the fastest sales cycle (days, not months), the highest pain intensity (owner does everything personally), and the lowest friction to close. One LinkedIn message + one call is enough. At €295/month, the decision is beneath the level of deliberation for any agency earning €200K+ per year. Build 15–20 proof-of-concept clients fast, then use those case studies to move up-market to mid-size agencies at €495–695/month.

City-by-City Rollout Strategy
Key principle: Build deep in one city before expanding. The report is most valuable when the data is hyper-local and the agent knows TrendsOnFire understands their specific market. Start with Utrecht (proof of concept already live), then expand market by market. Each new city requires ~4 hours to build the data layer; the report template reuses automatically.

The "Lindy approach" — what worked: Reference their existing LinkedIn content specifically. Don't pitch — offer to show them something already built for their agency. Send the live dashboard link + sample report before the call. By the time they get on the call, they've already seen the product. The call is 20 minutes, not a pitch — it's a "does this fit your workflow?" conversation.

Objection: "I already use NVM data"

NVM data is national quarterly summaries. The TrendsOnFire report is monthly, city-specific, neighbourhood-level, with overbidding analysis, mortgage rate tracking, buyer origin intelligence, seasonal timing, and ECB scenario modelling — plus your own performance benchmarked against the market. NVM data is an ingredient. This is the finished meal.
