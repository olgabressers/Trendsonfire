Interview Brief — Lead Omnichannel Operations Marketer · Partner Marketing

Booking.com: one journey for 4 million partners

$26.9B revenue. 4M accommodation partners across 220 countries. 87% non-hotel. Partner communications running on separate channels. The role: build a unified partner journey from fragmented infrastructure — during a $400–450M transformation programme.

Sources: Booking Holdings 10-K FY2024, 8-K FY2025, SEC filings, news.booking.com, partner.booking.com Updated 14 June 2026
Booking.com campus, Oosterdokseiland, Amsterdam · Hufton+Crow / UNStudio
Key conclusions
The company
Booking Holdings' largest brand. $26.9B revenue (+13% FY2025). 4M accommodation partners across 220 countries. 87% are homes, apartments and unique stays — not hotel operators. 1.2B room nights in 2025.
The role
Lead Omnichannel Operations Marketer — build a unified partner journey across email, Pulse push and extranet notifications, currently running as three independent channels with no shared partner context or coordinated send logic. Define omnichannel strategy, set up channel governance, translate business requirements into Salesforce Marketing Cloud and product delivery, and drive partner engagement through testing and KPI measurement.
Programme context
$550M run-rate savings achieved by end 2025. In 2026, $700M reinvested into product and partner capabilities. Partner marketing sits inside this context: more partner activation at flat or reduced headcount.
Key opportunity
87% of Booking's 4M partners are non-hotel — homes, apartments, and unique stays. Partner marketing was built for hotel operators. Three separate channels (email, Extranet, Pulse) run without shared partner context. The opportunity is to rebuild partner campaign design for the non-hotel majority and unify the journey across all channels.

Competitive Context
Key competitors — revenue evolution & positioning

Booking Holdings is the largest OTA globally — $23.7B revenue in 2024, growing 39% since 2022 vs Expedia's 15%. Trip.com is the only competitor growing faster (+150% same period), expanding beyond its China base. Booking held marketing flat at $6.8B across 2023–2024 while revenue grew, pushing its efficiency ratio to 29%; in 2025 it reinvested ($8.2B, +20%) into social and brand channels. Expedia spent 52% of revenue on marketing and grew three times slower. The implication for partner marketing: per-dollar efficiency pressure means higher weight on partner programme participation rates and promotional contribution — not more budget.

Role Analysis
3 structural challenges this role is built to solve
01
Channel fragmentation
  • Email, push, and in-app operate independently with no shared partner context or coordinated send logic
  • A partner managing multiple properties receives separate, uncoordinated communication across all three channels simultaneously
  • The opportunity: move from three independently managed channels to one integrated partner journey with shared context and governed send logic
Source: partner.booking.com · Booking.com partner communications infrastructure
02
Hotel-first campaigns reaching 87% non-hotel partners
  • 3.5M of 4M partners are homes, apartments, and unique stays — not hotels
  • In Q1 2025, alternative accommodations were 37% of all room nights and growing at 12% vs 7% overall
  • Central partner campaigns designed for professional hotel operators reach the majority of partners with wrong content and wrong cadence
Source: Q1 2025 earnings 8-K, SEC
03
No omnichannel governance model
  • 4M partners across 220 countries receive communications through three channels with no unified governance — no shared trigger logic, frequency caps, or channel conflict rules
  • No consistent KPI framework connecting channel activity to partner outcomes — each channel measures its own metrics independently
  • Result: partners are over-messaged, communications are not personalised to partner type or lifecycle stage, and no single view exists of what a partner has received across all channels
Source: partner.booking.com · Booking.com partner communications infrastructure
Transformation Programme — 2025 status: Launched November 2024 with an initial target of $400–450M in annual run-rate savings (workforce ~⅓, process modernisation and procurement ~⅔). By Q3 2025, Booking Holdings raised guidance to $500–550M based on stronger-than-expected early results. At year-end 2025, the company had enabled approximately $550M in annual run-rate savings — the top of its raised guidance range. In 2026, the programme is expected to deliver $500–550M in in-year savings, enabling $700M in reinvestment into product and partner capabilities. Partner marketing sits inside this context: scale and automate partner engagement with flat or reduced headcount while reallocating budget toward high-ROI channels and programme adoption. Sources: 8-K/A Nov 2024 (programme launch) · 8-K Q3 2025 (target raised to $500–550M) · Q4 2025 earnings call, Feb 2026 ($550M achieved)

Priority stack — 5-step approach
# Action Why first Output
1 Audit partner journey touchpoints by channel, region, and partner type (hotel vs non-hotel) Can't build one journey without knowing what exists — and where channels overlap or conflict today Touchpoint inventory by channel, region, and partner type
2 Define channel governance — ownership, triggers, content rules, frequency caps, conflict resolution Governance is the prerequisite for consistency. Without it, every channel team keeps optimising in isolation Operating framework; sign-off from channel leads and tech
3 Segment the partner base by type and lifecycle stage (onboarding, active, at-risk, dormant) Segment-specific journeys outperform generic ones. 3.5M non-hotel partners are underserved by hotel-first campaigns Segmentation taxonomy; CRM data requirements per segment
4 Build and run the first unified pilot — onboarding journey, one region, all channels coordinated Proves the model works before scaling globally. Gives regional teams a concrete template to adapt Pilot results vs. baseline; stakeholder case for rollout
5 Scale the unified programme and embed continuous improvement — extend the pilot across all partner segments, set KPI cadence, A/B testing rhythm, and feedback loop to product and content Pilot proves the model; scale is where partner impact and communication KPIs actually move. 04
KPIs
Full KPI framework — five layers
North Star — business impact
Partner activation rate
% of new partners completing onboarding and taking their first booking within 30 days of listing
Influenced room nights
Incremental bookings attributable to partner marketing programmes vs. a control group (holdout test)
Channel performance — health of individual communications
Email open rate & CTR
Click-to-open rate is more diagnostic than open rate alone — isolates content quality from deliverability
Push tap-through rate
% of push notifications tapped. Opt-out rate is the warning signal — rising opt-outs = wrong targeting or frequency
In-app engagement rate
Interaction rate on extranet in-app messages and notifications — closest to the partner at the point of action
Deliverability & opt-out
Bounce rate + unsubscribe rate. These go up when over-messaging starts — the first canary in a fragmented channel model
Journey performance — does the unified flow work
Journey completion rate
% of partners completing a given journey vs. dropping out. Each drop-off point is a specific fix
Conversion rate per stage
% taking the intended action at each journey step: activate listing, add photos, enrol in Genius, adopt a new feature
Time-to-first-booking
Days from partner signup to first confirmed booking — a direct measure of onboarding journey effectiveness
Channel attribution
Which channel drove the action in a multi-channel journey — required to allocate budget and optimise the mix
Partner engagement — long-term health
Programme adoption rate
% of partners enrolling in Genius, Preferred Programme, or other central initiatives after a marketing push
Partner CSAT / NPS
Satisfaction score from accommodation partners. Sustained positive stakeholder feedback is a core success measure for the programme manager role
Retention & reactivation
% of partners active vs. dormant. Reactivation rate measures whether win-back journeys work
Engagement depth
Number of features or programmes a partner uses — a proxy for how deeply embedded in the Booking.com ecosystem they are
Operational — omnichannel programme execution
Governance compliance rate
% of channels operating within the defined governance model — correct triggers, frequency caps, and content rules applied across email, push, and in-app
Test velocity
Number of A/B tests run per month across channels and journey stages — the operational metric for continuous improvement cadence
Stakeholder alignment score
Rating from business, channel, and tech stakeholders on quality of collaboration and decision speed — measures how well the role bridges across the organisation
Time-to-deploy
Cycle time from business requirement to live campaign in Salesforce Marketing Cloud — measures the efficiency of the business-to-tech translation process
