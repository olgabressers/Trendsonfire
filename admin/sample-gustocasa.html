Section 5: Energy Label Premium Analysis

New ⭐ Premium
Urgency Signal: In Utrecht, 34% of pre-1975 housing stock still carries label E, F or G. Sellers of these properties face an average €62,000 discount vs comparable label C homes — and this gap is widening as the 2033 EU minimum label C deadline approaches.
Energy Label Price Premium/Discount vs Label C Baseline
Source: CBS Energielabels dataset [10], NVM Energy Premium Analysis [1]
A label A property may achieve an 18% premium while a label G property could sell at a 28% discount vs an equivalent label C home. On a €500K property, the swing between A and G may represent up to €230,000 in value.
E/F/G Label Discount Gap — Growing Over Time
Source: PBL Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency [11], CBS [10]
The average discount for E/F/G label properties has grown from 8% in 2022 to an estimated 21% in 2026 — and is likely to accelerate further as regulatory deadlines tighten. Advise sellers to renovate before listing where feasible.
Strategic Opportunity for GustoCasa: GustoCasa can position as the advisor who helps sellers navigate energy label improvements before listing — capturing renovation referral fees and achieving higher eventual sale prices. A pre-listing energy audit service could be a significant differentiator and revenue stream as the 2033 deadline approaches.
