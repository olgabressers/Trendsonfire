Section 7
Market Signals & 90-Day Outlook

Three key signals for GustoCasa's strategy in March–May 2026.

🟢
Opportunity
Buyer demand stays strong

14.3 seekers per available property sustains a seller-favourable market. Overbidding at 12.2% gives sellers confidence. Spring viewing season (March–May) typically adds 15–20% to transaction volume.

🟡
Watch
Seller anxiety creating urgency

Media coverage of rate pressure and "cooling" is making sellers nervous — despite strong data. Agencies leading with data-backed confidence will win mandates from hesitant sellers who might otherwise wait. GustoCasa's 9.9% outperformance is the answer.

🔴
Risk
Rate sensitivity at €521K avg

At the current avg price, a 50bp rate increase reduces buyer purchasing power by €35,000–€45,000. ECB May meeting is key. Buyers in the €450K–€600K bracket (GustoCasa's sweet spot) are rate-sensitive. Educate them early.

Data Sources
Sources & Methodology

All data in this report is sourced from publicly available official and professional databases. No proprietary paid data subscriptions are required to compile this report monthly.

[1]
NVM — Nederlandse Coöperatieve Vereniging van Makelaars
Transaction volumes, average prices, overbidding percentages, buyer agent penetration. Published quarterly in the NVM Kwartaalcijfers and Vastgoedmonitor. NVM members also have access to regional breakdowns via the NVM Data & Research portal.
URL: nvm.nl/marktinformatie — Free with NVM membership; public summary data available without login.
[2]
Funda Marktinzicht
Weekly active seeker counts and available property counts by region. This is the primary real-time supply/demand signal for Dutch residential markets. Updated every Thursday.
URL: funda.nl/marktinzicht — Free to access; regional filters available for Utrecht.
[3]
Kadaster — Koopprijsstatistiek & BAG Transactiedata
Official land registry data. Transaction-level neighbourhood prices, price per m², sold property volumes. Source of record for Dutch property transactions.
URL: kadaster.nl — Koopprijsstatistiek — Monthly publication; free summaries, paid data for granular postcode-level analysis.
[4]
CBS StatLine — Centraal Bureau voor de Statistiek
Population growth, household formation, income data, and housing ownership statistics for Utrecht municipality and province. Used for context on structural demand drivers.
URL: CBS StatLine — Eigenwoningmarkt — Fully free; open data.
[5]
Dutch Lender Rate Cards — Direct Sources
Mortgage rates published directly by: ABN AMRO, ING, Rabobank, SNS Bank, Aegon, Nationale Nederlanden, Florius, BLG Wonen. Cross-referenced via Independer.nl and Hypotheker.nl for consistency.
All rates are publicly listed on lender websites and updated in real time. Collection takes approximately 15 minutes monthly.
[6]
Colegio de Registradores de España — Estadística Registral Inmobiliaria
Official Spanish property registry. Transaction volumes, foreign buyer percentages, price per m² by province. Quarterly publication.
URL: registradores.org/estadisticas/inmobiliario — Free; quarterly report PDF download.
[7]
TINSA — Índice de Mercados Inmobiliarios Españoles (IMIE)
Spanish property price index by region and city type. Leading private source for Spanish residential price trends, used by banks and media.
URL: tinsa.es/informes-estadisticas — Monthly free summaries; full data via subscription.
[8]
Ministerio de Transportes, Movilidad y Agenda Urbana (Spain)
Official Spanish government housing statistics. New construction permits, transaction counts, average prices by autonomous community and province.
URL: mitma.gob.es — Estadística de Transacciones Inmobiliarias — Free; quarterly.
[9]
GustoCasa Proprietary Transaction Data
