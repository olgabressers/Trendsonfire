What TrendsOnFire Actually Is
TrendsOnFire is a macro/strategic market intelligence platform. All data comes from public statistical sources: CBS, Kadaster, Eurostat, ECB, ONS, and equivalent national agencies. Reports are monthly or quarterly. They cover city-level and national-level trends, price trajectories, demographic shifts, ECB rate scenarios, and EU comparisons.

This is not a real-time transaction feed. It does not replicate NVM/Brainbay data. It does not provide individual listing data, overbid ratios by street, or days-on-market for a specific agency. Clients who need that level of operational detail are not the right fit.
Lesson from GustoCasa (April 2026): GustoCasa declined because they already have NVM/Brainbay transaction data at a level we cannot match. Active real estate agencies need operational, near-real-time data. TrendsOnFire provides strategic, macro data. These are different products for different buyers. Never pitch real estate agencies on data freshness or granularity.

Client Segments — Who Buys This and Why

Real Estate Investors

Private equity · Family offices · Buy-to-let portfolios

Make long-term capital allocation decisions across cities and countries. Need macro price trajectories, yield trends, regulatory risk, and ECB rate forecasts. Do not need daily transaction feeds — they need the big picture 3–5 years out.

  • City-level price trend reports (quarterly)
  • EU country comparison for capital allocation
  • ECB rate scenario modelling — impact on yields
  • Housing shortage vs new construction gap analysis
  • Rental yield benchmarking across cities
Priority: HIGH — data fits perfectly · Decision cycle: weeks

HR & Corporate Relocation

Multinationals · ASML, Shell, KPMG, Booking.com · Relocation agencies

Companies relocating international staff need housing market briefings: which city to settle people, what rents and purchase prices look like, cost-of-living trajectory, and which neighbourhoods are realistic on an expat salary. HR teams don't have time to build this themselves.

  • City-by-city comparison: Amsterdam vs Utrecht vs Eindhoven
  • Expat housing cost briefings (rent + purchase)
  • Cost-of-living trend analysis
  • Quarterly update: has the market shifted since we placed last batch?
  • EU briefings for cross-border relocations
Priority: HIGH — recurring need · Budget sits in HR/facilities · Annual contract

Property Developers

Project developers · Woningcorporaties · Area development

Plan projects 2–5 years out. Need population growth projections, housing shortage forecasts, planning pipeline, and price band analysis for the segment they are building in. CBS and PBL data is ideal — but packaged, not raw. They buy time saved, not data they don't have.

  • Market feasibility briefings per city or neighbourhood
  • Population & household growth projections
  • Housing shortage gap (demand vs supply pipeline)
  • Price band distribution — what segment is undersupplied
  • Energy label requirements & new construction trends
Priority: MEDIUM — longer sales cycle · Higher contract value

Consultancies & Strategy Firms

McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, boutique real estate advisors

When working on real estate, market entry, or urban development mandates, consultants need packaged market intelligence they can use in client deliverables. They would rather buy a white-label briefing than build from scratch — especially for markets outside their primary expertise.

  • White-label country or city briefings
  • EU market comparison packs for market entry work
  • Data snapshots for slide decks and client reports
  • Ongoing subscription for teams covering real estate
Priority: MEDIUM — strong fit · Reach via LinkedIn or direct intro

Multinationals Entering NL or EU

US / Asian companies setting up European HQ

Companies choosing between Amsterdam, Dublin, Luxembourg, or Frankfurt for their European base need comparative real estate, labour market, and cost-of-living intelligence. A one-time briefing pack or annual subscription covers their setup period.

  • City selection briefings: NL vs EU alternatives
  • Office market context (where staff will live)
  • Housing affordability vs salary benchmarks
  • Expat relocation cost modelling
Priority: MEDIUM — one-time or annual · Higher unit price

What Each Client Type Would Receive

Real estate investor — quarterly briefing

City-level price trend (CBS/Kadaster), rental yield benchmarking, housing shortage vs new construction pipeline, ECB rate scenarios and impact on affordability, population and migration inflow. 8–12 pages. Delivered first week of each quarter. Available in NL/EN. Branded under TrendsOnFire or white-labelled.

HR / relocation team — city comparison pack

Side-by-side: Amsterdam, Utrecht, Eindhoven, Rotterdam, Den Haag. For each: avg purchase price, avg rent (1–3 bed), price growth trajectory, commute context, expat concentration, energy cost index. One-time delivery or quarterly update. EN only. Can include EU cities on request (Eurostat-sourced).

Property developer — feasibility briefing

Market demand analysis for a specific city or segment: population growth to 2030, household formation, housing shortage gap, planning pipeline (CBS/RVO), price band distribution, energy label transition pace. Typically one-off with optional quarterly update. Higher price point justified by specificity.

LinkedIn content — monthly post series

Based on each CBS/Kadaster/Eurostat release: one post with a sharp data point, one visual (chart from TrendsOnFire), two-sentence takeaway, CTA to full briefing. Builds Olga's authority as a housing intelligence analyst. Drives inbound from all other segments over time.

How to Approach Prospects
Core principle — same as GustoCasa but for the right clients: Never cold pitch. First demonstrate the product. Post on LinkedIn → they see it. Then send a short message: "I built a market briefing relevant to your team — would you like to see it?" Attach a sample relevant to their sector and city. The product closes itself once seen by the right audience.
