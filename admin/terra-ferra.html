02 Business Model

What is Terra Ferra? A platform where emerging fashion designers sell limited collections to their fans — before the items are even made. Fans place orders in advance, the designer uses that money to produce the collection, and fans who helped spread the word earn a share of the sales. Terra Ferra provides the technology and handles all payments. It earns a fee on every sale.

Model 1

Marketplace

Terra Ferra builds its own buyer audience and sends customers to designers. Like Etsy or Depop — you list your product, the platform finds the buyer.

→ Platform pays for customer acquisition
→ Slower and more expensive to launch
→ Higher platform value once scaled
Revenue: 7% commission on sales · Comparable: Etsy, Depop, Vinted
Model 2 · Current direction

Collaboration tool

Designers bring their own fans from Instagram or TikTok. Terra Ferra gives them tools to run pre-sales and superfan earnings with that existing audience.

→ Designer brings the customers
→ Faster and cheaper to launch
→ Platform value tied to designer success
Revenue: 7% commission + $75/month subscription · Comparable: Patreon, Spring
Which model is Terra Ferra building?

The 7% fee on pre-sale orders and the fan earnings mechanic both point to Model 2 as the right starting point. The two models are not mutually exclusive — once the platform has designers and active fans, adding a public marketplace layer becomes a natural second step.

Who are the players and what does each one get
Designer
An emerging fashion creator with an existing audience
Example: a designer with 15,000 Instagram followers who makes limited-edition streetwear but has no way to fund or pre-sell collections.

What they get: tools to run pre-sales, build community, and receive upfront funding before making a single item.
Superfan
A loyal follower who goes beyond buying — they back the designer financially
Example: a fan commits €100 before the collection exists, as a pre-sale pledge. If the collection sells out, she earns 7% on every sale she helped drive through her own social sharing.

What they earn: 7% royalty on sales they generate. First €50 earned typically triggers word-of-mouth. This is the growth engine.
Terra Ferra
The platform connecting both sides and handling the money
Provides the storefront, pre-sale mechanics, payment processing, and superfan earnings dashboard.

What it earns: 7% commission on completed sales + 7% on pre-sale commitments + $75/month from the designer for using the platform.

The White Space

Every platform left of centre treats the community as a buyer. Every platform right of centre (Patreon, Spring) lets creators earn from their community — but none do it in fashion, and none make the community itself a co-investor in product launches. Terra Ferra's top-right position is unoccupied by any scaled competitor. That is the strategic case.

06 What This Role Requires

Terra Ferra is not a management challenge — it is an execution challenge. The model is defined, the founder has deep domain experience, and the market timing is right. What the company needs now is an operator who can translate vision into working machinery. The three requirements below define the brief.

The role requires Experience I bring
→Marketplace & platform operations Commercial and digital operations across e-commerce and omni-channel businesses — building the operational infrastructure that connects supply (designers) to demand (superfans) at scale.
→Community & partner programme management Building and running partner ecosystems — from onboarding and enablement to retention. The designer relationship is a partner relationship; the superfan programme is a loyalty and affiliate operation. Both are familiar territory.
→Startup-to-scale transformation Experience taking organisations from early-stage ambiguity to structured, measurable operation — defining the KPIs, building the team, and creating the reporting that lets a founder stay in the vision lane while the company scales.
→Fundraising readiness Building the numbers story that investors need: sales growth, how many designers stay and grow, whether fans recruit other fans. These numbers do not exist yet — they get built by running the platform.
→European market access Based in the Netherlands, with a European commercial network. Fashion is a European category — AMFI Amsterdam, Antwerp, Paris, London. That network is a direct acquisition channel for designers and an expansion story for the investor deck.
The Working Model

Maxim Nogotkov's operating track record — Svyaznoy, Pandora Russia, Enter — shows a founder who is exceptional at identifying a structural market opportunity and building it fast. Terra Ferra is that same instinct applied to fashion and the creator economy. What it needs alongside that is an operator who ensures the day-to-day machinery runs: designer onboarding, superfan activation, investor reporting, team building. That is the CEO role. The founder leads vision and fundraising strategy. The CEO runs the company.

Terra Ferra's first $315K in 2022 was at the lower end of what a proper build required. Why the range is wide — and what narrows it

The difference between a $12M and a $65M company is one variable: whether the superfan co-investor model creates genuine network effects. If superfans recruit other superfans because they are visibly earning — the platform grows without paid acquisition. If it does not, it is a standard niche marketplace. The first 18 months of live operation will answer that question. The base scenario ($20–32M) is achievable with disciplined execution and no viral component.
