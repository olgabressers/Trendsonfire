What the platform does: Terra Ferra connects emerging fashion designers with communities of "superfans" — prosumers who co-create, pre-buy, and share in revenue. Designers get a fully equipped launchpad covering team assembly, community building, crowdfunded pre-sales, and distribution. Superfans earn a 7% royalty on sales they drive. The platform takes 7% on sales and 7% on pre-sale commitments.

The model targets three structural problems in early-stage fashion: high barriers to starting a brand, expensive community building, and limited access to non-dilutive funding. The proposed solution — turning buyers into co-investors — is distinct from Kickstarter's reward model and more closely resembles equity crowdfunding applied to fashion product launches.

Strategic Observation

Terra Ferra raised its pre-seed in early 2022 and has been in closed beta for over three years. In that period the broader creator economy has matured, social commerce has gone mainstream, and the window for first-mover advantage in this specific intersection — community-owned fashion pre-sales — has not closed, but it is narrowing. The platform's moment is now.

The White Space

Every platform left of centre treats the community as a buyer. Every platform right of centre (Patreon, Spring) lets creators earn from their community — but none do it in fashion, and none make the community itself a co-investor in product launches. Terra Ferra's top-right position is unoccupied by any scaled competitor. That is the strategic case.

03 Opportunities & Gaps

Terra Ferra has a genuinely differentiated model. The four gaps below are not reasons to walk away — they are the operating brief for the incoming CEO. Each is solvable with the right operational leadership in place.

Gap 1 · Launch

Three years in beta — no public launch

The platform completed a closed beta with 10 designers and approximately 50 users by early 2022, and has not publicly launched since. The website is currently unreachable. Without a live product, every other initiative — fundraising, designer acquisition, superfan growth — is blocked. Launch is the prerequisite.

Gap 2 · Strategic Focus

Pivot signals suggest model uncertainty

Recent platform descriptions have shifted from "community marketplace" toward "fashion influencer marketing agency." This may reflect a pragmatic revenue-generating step — or an unresolved debate about what the business actually is. The CEO role requires resolving that question and committing to a clear go-to-market.

Gap 3 · Fundraising

$3M SAFE target unconfirmed after three years

The $315K pre-seed from Q1 2022 funded early product work. The follow-on $3M SAFE has not been publicly confirmed. A professional CEO can build the operational metrics story — GMV, designer retention, superfan activation — that investors need to underwrite the next round.

Opportunity · Market Timing

Creator economy is now infrastructure, not trend

In 2021 the creator economy was a VC thesis. In 2026 it is $250B+ globally and growing. Emerging designers have audiences but no monetisation infrastructure. Brands need community-led distribution. Terra Ferra's model is better positioned today than it was at founding — it just needs operational execution to capture it.

04 Three CEO Priorities
1
Priority One · Months 1–6

Commercial launch: from 10 designers in beta to 100 on the market

The first task is defining who Terra Ferra is for and taking the platform live. That means an ideal designer profile — established micro-brand, 5K–50K Instagram followers, proven demand but not yet funded — and a structured onboarding that moves a designer from application to live storefront in 30 days.

Go-to-market channels: fashion school partnerships (FIT, AMFI, Parsons), pop-up activations at emerging designer shows, direct outreach to the top 200 Depop and Vinted sellers building their own brand. Each of these channels reaches the exact designer profile the model is built for.

Target: 100 active designers · Month 6 €250K GMV · Year 1 30-day onboarding cycle Platform live · Month 1
2
Priority Two · Months 3–9

Activate the community flywheel — superfans who earn become superfans who recruit

The differentiating mechanic is the superfan-as-co-investor model. Kickstarter, Depop, and Instagram Shopping treat buyers as customers. Terra Ferra treats them as stakeholders. That is a real competitive moat — but only if superfans are actively earning and talking about it.

Priority two is operationalising that mechanic: a tiered ambassador system (buyer → earner → advocate → co-creator), transparent earnings dashboards, and social proof content from superfans showing their first payouts. The moment a superfan earns their first €50 and posts about it, Terra Ferra has its acquisition engine.

Target: 10,000 active superfans · Year 1 Superfan activation rate > 40% Tiered ambassador program live Avg. earnings per superfan tracked
3
Priority Three · Months 6–12

Build the Series A metrics story — three numbers that close the $3M round

Investors in marketplace businesses fund three things: GMV growth rate, retention (do designers stay and grow?), and the defensibility of the network (do superfans bring more superfans?). Right now those three numbers are not publicly visible. Priority three is creating them.

That means clean financial tracking, a monthly metrics dashboard, and a fundraising narrative built around the unique mechanic: "the only fashion platform where fans are co-investors, not just buyers." The $3M SAFE is the bridge to a Series A at meaningful traction. With the right metrics in place, that conversation becomes fundable.

05 What This Role Requires

Terra Ferra is not a management challenge — it is an execution challenge. The model is defined, the founder has deep domain experience, and the market timing is right. What the company needs now is an operator who can translate vision into working machinery. The three requirements below define the brief.

The role requires Experience I bring
→Marketplace & platform operations Commercial and digital operations across e-commerce and omni-channel businesses — building the operational infrastructure that connects supply (designers) to demand (superfans) at scale.
→Community & partner programme management Building and running partner ecosystems — from onboarding and enablement to retention. The designer relationship is a partner relationship; the superfan programme is a loyalty and affiliate operation. Both are familiar territory.
→Startup-to-scale transformation Experience taking organisations from early-stage ambiguity to structured, measurable operation — defining the KPIs, building the team, and creating the reporting that lets a founder stay in the vision lane while the company scales.
→Fundraising readiness Building the business case and operational narrative that investors fund. The Series A story is a metrics story — and metrics are what an operator builds.
→European market access Based in the Netherlands, with a European commercial network. Fashion is a European category — AMFI Amsterdam, Antwerp, Paris, London. That network is a direct acquisition channel for designers and an expansion story for the investor deck.
The Working Model

Maxim Nogotkov's operating track record — Svyaznoy, Pandora Russia, Enter — shows a founder who is exceptional at identifying a structural market opportunity and building it fast. Terra Ferra is that same instinct applied to fashion and the creator economy. What it needs alongside that is an operator who ensures the day-to-day machinery runs: designer onboarding, superfan activation, investor reporting, team building. That is the CEO role. The founder leads vision and fundraising strategy. The CEO runs the company.
