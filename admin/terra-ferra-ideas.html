Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
Full Attribution Transparency — Build It In from Day One
ShopMy is valued at $1.5B precisely because it shows brands exactly which ambassador drove each sale. LTK withholds this and is now struggling to pivot. Every TF designer should see in real time which ambassador and which superfan drove which order. This is the structural differentiator that makes designers stay and pay.
02
ShopMy "Circles" → TF Co-Creation Feed
ShopMy's consumer app shows curated collections from creators the user follows. TF's version: curated drops by designers + the superfan community that co-created them — the design story as content, not just a product listing. ShopMy proves this consumer layer is where the next billion in GMV comes from. Plan it from the start, not as Phase 2.
03
Ablo's AI Design Tool for TF Designers
TF targets designers at ~$1M revenue, 5K–50K followers — exactly the stage where they have creative vision but limited technical design capability. Ablo Studio (or similar API) could be integrated as TF's AI design tool: sketch-to-digital, on-demand prototyping. Removes the friction between creative idea and shareable pre-order prototype.
04
Financial Royalties for Co-Creators, Not Just Credit
Ablo auto-distributes royalties when a design is remixed. TF's co-creation gives named credit — this should also be financial. When a superfan's colour or silhouette input is used in the final collection, an automatic royalty (cash or platform credit) flows to them. Turns a loyalty mechanic into a genuine economic stake.
05
Premium Curation Over Volume
ShopMy wins with 1,200 premium brands vs LTK's 5,000 mass-market brands. TF should resist onboarding any designer who applies. A curated, high-trust designer roster protects consumer experience and brand perception. Selective = premium. This is also TF's only realistic competitive angle against LTK's scale advantage.
06
The Consumer App Is the Flywheel, Not Phase 2
ShopMy built B2B tools first, then launched "Circles" consumer app in July 2025 and immediately validated three-sided model economics. TF's consumer taste profile, wishlist, and co-creation feed should be designed from day one. The designers need the consumer-side data to justify staying on TF vs going direct to Instagram.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
ML-Powered Style Matching: Designer ↔ Influencer
Syte.ai's visual embedding approach (or CLIP-based open-source alternative) can encode both a designer's portfolio and an influencer's feed into the same aesthetic vector space — then rank influencers by style fit score, not just follower count. Core use case: finding ambassadors and designers who share the same visual DNA (e.g. street style, coastal minimalist) without relying on manual category tags.
02
Visual Search in the TF Consumer App
Syte.ai API integration: consumer photographs a look they spotted IRL or on social media → TF surfaces the pre-orders from TF designers with matching aesthetic. Turns passive discovery into active pre-order intent. No large existing catalog needed — even 10–20 active designers provide enough signal to make this useful at launch.
03
TF's Own Data IS the Trend Intelligence Product
Heuritech and Stylumia charge brands enterprise SaaS rates (pricing undisclosed) for trend signals derived from public social media. TF's engagement data (VTO sessions, co-creation votes, wishlist saves, pre-order conversion by style) is first-party demand intent — more valuable and more actionable. TF should not buy Heuritech; TF should eventually compete with it for the "data as revenue" stream.
04
"Trending in Your Niche" Designer Alerts
Even without licensing Heuritech, TF can build a lightweight version using its own co-creation votes and save data: notify each designer when a colour, silhouette, or occasion type in their portfolio is gaining traction relative to 30-day baseline. Positions TF as a strategic intelligence partner to designers, not just a sales channel.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
The Platform That Doesn't Exist Yet in Europe
Wolf & Badger has independent designer curation. Depop has the Gen Z community mechanics and social discovery. Neither has co-creation + pre-order + revenue sharing with the community. TF is the combination that doesn't yet exist in Europe — and both W&B and Depop's growth numbers show the appetite is there.
02
Wolf & Badger's Subscription Blueprint
W&B charges designers $375/month + undisclosed commission and generated ~$50M revenue at $95M GMV — implying a ~52% take rate including subscription. TF's model (7% GMV commission, no listing fee) is structurally lower cost for designers. TF can position this directly: same curated designer marketplace, lower fees, plus a co-creation community that W&B does not have.
03
Depop's Fee Pivot Validates TF's Revenue Model
Depop eliminated seller fees in July 2024 and saw GMV grow +31.6% in the same year. This is a live A/B test confirming: removing seller friction grows the platform faster than extracting fees upfront. TF's GMV-only revenue model (no listing fees, commission only on completed sales) is now backed by Depop's real-world outcome, not just theory.
