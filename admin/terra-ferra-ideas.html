Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
Real-time visibility of ambassador, superfan, order — phase 1 priority
Same attribution model as ShopMy. LTK doesn't have it and is losing designers because of it. Include in phase 1 product scope.
02
Consumer feed: the story behind the drop, not just the product
Designer + the superfans who shaped it + the community vote. ShopMy launched Circles in July 2025 for this reason. Include the consumer layer in phase 1 — without it, designers have no reason to stay on TF over posting direct on Instagram.
03
AI design tool: sketch → shareable pre-order prototype
TF's target designers have vision but no production tools. Ablo Studio API: sketch in, digital prototype out, ready to share and pre-order. Lower the barrier to getting a collection live on TF.
04
Superfan royalties — cash, not just a credit
Ablo auto-pays royalties when a design is remixed. TF gives named credit today — add a payout mechanic. Superfan's colour choice ships in the collection, they get a cut. Cash or platform credit.
05
Define designer selection criteria before launch
ShopMy: 1,200 brands. LTK: 5,000. ShopMy is winning on quality. Put a selection doc in place before onboarding opens — every low-quality designer accepted dilutes the platform for everyone else.
06
Consumer app: include in phase 1 product scope
Consumer engagement data is what keeps designers on TF instead of their own channels. ShopMy built B2B first and added the consumer layer in 2025 — a multi-year delay. Both sides need to be in the initial product scope.

The EU Market Gap
The Gap
No European platform combines co-creation, pre-order, and community revenue sharing. TF's model does not exist here yet.

W&B ($95M GMV, profitable) and NOTHS (~$110M revenue) prove the independent designer market is real in Europe — but both are pure marketplaces. Designer produces, consumer buys. That's it.

Wolf & Badger
wolfandbadger.com
What it doesCurated marketplace for 2,000+ independent designers — fashion (83%), jewellery, beauty, homeware — no inventory held; brands self-manage listings
Founded2010 — London, UK (founders: George & Henry Graham)
Funding~£4.5M Series A (2019, Guinness Asset Management); ~$20M total per media; B Corp certified
Creators2,000+ independent designer brands; ~300 applications per month
GMV~$95M (2024)
Revenue~$50M (2024) — second consecutive profitable year
Revenue growthUS grew from $3M (2019) to $45M+ (2024); US now ~50% of total sales
BrandsN/A — designers are the product (no separate brand client tier)
ModelMonthly designer subscription + undisclosed % commission per sale; stores in London, NYC, LA
CommissionsNot publicly disclosed — set individually per brand in seller dashboard. Subscription: $375/month per brand (source: Modern Retail, W&B seller terms)
GeographyLondon HQ · NYC (SoHo, since 2017) · LA (West Hollywood, since 2022) — US ~50% of sales
StrengthProfitable independent designer marketplace: $95M GMV, $50M revenue, 2,000+ curated brands with no inventory risk. Proves US market is reachable for a European-founded independent designer platform — US grew from $3M to $45M+ in five years.
WeaknessNo co-creation, no community mechanics, no pre-order or crowdfunding — pure marketplace. $375/month subscription fee raises the barrier for early-stage designers. Mixed categories dilute fashion community focus.
Overlap with TFCurated independent designers only (not mass market). Asset-light — no platform inventory. Premium positioning. US + UK market presence. Designer-as-brand model.
Gap TF fillsW&B has no co-creation — consumers buy what designers already made. No pre-order — designers produce first, then list. No community revenue sharing. No superfan or ambassador layer. W&B's $375/month subscription + implied ~52% take rate vs. TF's 7% GMV commission is a direct recruitment argument for TF when approaching W&B's 2,000+ designer base.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
EU investor pitch: $200M market, co-creation not built yet
W&B + NOTHS together generate $200M+ in EU independent designer revenue, both profitable. Neither platform has co-creation, pre-order, or community revenue sharing. TF fills that gap — use as the EU market proof point in fundraising.
02
7,000 warm designer leads in W&B and NOTHS seller bases
Already selling online, already paying 25–52% commission. TF offer: 7% + co-creation tools + pre-order. Direct outreach, no cold pipeline needed.
03
EU entry point: UK first
Both competitors are UK-based. 4M+ consumers already buying from independent designers online. Designer base and buyer behaviour already in place. Start here.
