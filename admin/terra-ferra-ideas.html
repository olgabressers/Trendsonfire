Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
Full Attribution Transparency — Build It In from Day One
ShopMy is valued at $1.5B precisely because it shows brands exactly which ambassador drove each sale. LTK withholds this and is now struggling to pivot. Every TF designer should see in real time which ambassador and which superfan drove which order. This is the structural differentiator that makes designers stay and pay.
02
ShopMy "Circles" → TF Co-Creation Feed
ShopMy's consumer app shows curated collections from creators the user follows. TF's version: curated drops by designers + the superfan community that co-created them — the design story as content, not just a product listing. ShopMy proves this consumer layer is where the next billion in GMV comes from. Plan it from the start, not as Phase 2.
03
Ablo's AI Design Tool for TF Designers
TF targets designers at ~$1M revenue, 5K–50K followers — exactly the stage where they have creative vision but limited technical design capability. Ablo Studio (or similar API) could be integrated as TF's AI design tool: sketch-to-digital, on-demand prototyping. Removes the friction between creative idea and shareable pre-order prototype.
04
Financial Royalties for Co-Creators, Not Just Credit
Ablo auto-distributes royalties when a design is remixed. TF's co-creation gives named credit — this should also be financial. When a superfan's colour or silhouette input is used in the final collection, an automatic royalty (cash or platform credit) flows to them. Turns a loyalty mechanic into a genuine economic stake.
05
Premium Curation Over Volume
ShopMy wins with 1,200 premium brands vs LTK's 5,000 mass-market brands. TF should resist onboarding any designer who applies. A curated, high-trust designer roster protects consumer experience and brand perception. Selective = premium. This is also TF's only realistic competitive angle against LTK's scale advantage.
06
The Consumer App Is the Flywheel, Not Phase 2
ShopMy built B2B tools first, then launched "Circles" consumer app in July 2025 and immediately validated three-sided model economics. TF's consumer taste profile, wishlist, and co-creation feed should be designed from day one. The designers need the consumer-side data to justify staying on TF vs going direct to Instagram.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
The Workflow: Lookbook In → Matched Ambassadors Out
Modash's free trial enables this from day one: upload a target designer's lookbook → surface creators posting in the same visual aesthetic → outreach to the top 10 as TF's first ambassador cohort. This is TF's designer onboarding engine before any proprietary matching is built. Validate the tool in the trial period, then decide whether $199/mo is justified by the quality of matches returned.
02
Archive Free Tier: Build the UGC Library from Day One
Archive captures every Instagram and TikTok post from tracked creators automatically. Set it up across TF's first ambassador cohort on launch day — every post, story, and reel gets indexed into TF's content library at no cost. This becomes the attribution baseline designers see in their dashboard: which ambassador posted what, when, and what it drove.
03
The Long-Term Moat: TF Builds Its Own Matching Signal
Modash and Archive match by visual similarity to reference images — they have no data on which aesthetic pairings actually convert on TF. As TF scales, co-creation votes, wishlist saves, and pre-order conversion by aesthetic become first-party matching signals no external tool has. The open-source FashionCLIP model (Hugging Face) provides the vector embedding layer for a proprietary matcher TF can build on top of its own data. Modash is the tool for today; TF's own signal is the competitive moat for tomorrow.
The EU Market Gap
The Gap
No European platform combines co-creation, pre-order, and community revenue sharing. TF's model does not exist here yet.

W&B ($95M GMV, profitable) and NOTHS (~$110M revenue) prove the independent designer market is real in Europe — but both are pure marketplaces. Designer produces, consumer buys. That's it.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
This Is the EU Fundraising Narrative
W&B and NOTHS together represent $200M+ in proven EU independent designer commerce revenue — with zero co-creation, pre-order, or community layer. TF's pitch to EU investors is not "can this market exist?" — it already does, at scale, profitably. The pitch is: the next layer hasn't been built yet, and TF is building it.
02
7,000 Pre-Vetted EU Designers — Acquisition Doesn't Start Cold
W&B's 2,000+ and NOTHS's 5,000+ independent designers are already active, curated, and digitally fluent — paying W&B's ~52% implied take rate or NOTHS's 25% + £199/year joining fee. TF's EU designer outreach starts with a direct cost argument: same independent designer model, lower fees, plus co-creation and pre-order tools neither platform offers.
03
UK Is the EU Entry Point
Both W&B and NOTHS are UK-first platforms. UK independent designer consumers already trust and regularly buy from curated independent designer marketplaces. TF enters a market with established buying behaviour — not a cold education problem. UK is the natural EU beachhead: English language, existing independent designer culture, and two large competitor platforms whose designer bases are directly recruitable.
