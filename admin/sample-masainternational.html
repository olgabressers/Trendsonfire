Insight 01 — Dutch Buyers

Dutch buyers are the #1 foreign buyer group in Alicante province, up from #2 in 2021. Volume grew 24% in the last two years — fuelled by remote work flexibility, early retirement, and the Amsterdam affordability exodus. This is MASA's core demographic and a structural tailwind. [6]

Insight 02 — German Acceleration

German buyers are accelerating: +19% in 2025. German media has driven a 'Costa Blanca moment' — multiple major German real estate portals added dedicated Spanish property sections in 2024. This creates a substantial new buyer pool MASA can strategically tap with German-language touchpoints. [6]

Insight 03 — UK Buyer Dynamics

UK buyer recovery post-Brexit is slower but stabilising. Key barrier: UK buyers now face non-EU purchase restrictions in some municipalities. This creates a direct opportunity — Dutch and German buyers face no EU ownership restrictions, making them structurally preferable for sellers and developers. [16]

Airport Infrastructure

Alicante airport handled 11.82M arrivals in 2025, up 8.4% YoY. The planned €1.2B airport expansion (approved 2024) targets 20M passengers by 2030 — a structural multiplier for Costa Blanca property demand and rental yields across the entire coast. [24]

Short-Term Rental Performance

Torrevieja short-term rentals run at 94% occupancy in August. Even during shoulder months (May, October), occupancy exceeds 70% — making it one of the highest-yielding Airbnb markets in southern Europe. Year-round demand is structurally higher than comparable French Riviera or Portuguese markets. [25]

Demand Structural Outlook

Northern European remote workers represent a new permanent demand segment — buyers who live in Spain 4–5 months per year rather than 2–3. This extends occupancy seasons, improves resale valuations, and creates a larger pool of buyers for MASA's €180–400K core inventory. [16]

Valencia Community (including Alicante Province) is actively reviewing short-term rental licensing caps. MASA should proactively advise buyer clients to verify tourist licence status — and confirm viability — before purchase. This is a material risk for investment buyers.
