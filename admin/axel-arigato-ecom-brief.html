The business in brief
Digital model
Founded online-only 2014. Drop-of-the-week (52 releases/year, not seasonal). Own DTC + luxury marketplaces (Farfetch, MyTheresa, SSENSE) + prestige wholesale (Harrods, Saks, Nordstrom). Online ≈ 2× all physical stores combined.
Where the brand is now
Returned to operating profit 2024. New CEO Frédéric Serrant (ex Adidas APAC, appointed Feb 2026). Core functions relocating London → Gothenburg. Actively building commercial leadership team.
This role
Head of E-commerce & Marketplaces. Reports to CCO. Gothenburg, on-site. Owns the P&L for the largest commercial channel in the business.

Strategic context — what changes in 2026

New CEO · Gothenburg build · Next growth phase

New CEO — Frédéric Serrant
Ex Adidas APAC Managing Director. 16 years at Adidas across APAC, Southeast Asia, Brazil, Chile. Appointed 3 February 2026. Thinks in channels, P&L, and scale — not founder intuition. The Head of E-commerce reports into a CCO who answers to this CEO. Commercial clarity and data-backed decisions are non-negotiable from day one.
London → Gothenburg relocation
Core departments moving from London to Gothenburg. The e-commerce team is being built in Gothenburg now. This is a build role, not a step-into role. The Head of E-commerce shapes the team structure, the tooling, and the operating model from the ground up.
Next growth phase
US entered Sept 2024 (NYC Soho). UAE entered Sept 2025 (Dubai, Chalhoub Group). Abu Dhabi and Jeddah planned. The e-commerce channel must serve markets ahead of store openings — digital demand validation precedes physical investment. This is how the brand has always operated. The Head of E-commerce owns the digital signal that tells the business where to open next.
