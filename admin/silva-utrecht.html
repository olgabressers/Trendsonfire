About the Silva Method

What is the Silva Method?

Developed by José Silva in the 1960s, the Silva Method is a self-help and personal development programme based on training the mind to function at the alpha brainwave level — a relaxed, meditative state in which learning, creativity, and intuition are said to be enhanced. It is one of the longest-running personal development programmes in the world, with millions of graduates in 110+ countries.

The programme is structured in two modules, each delivered over a 2-day live session. Ирина Хлимоненко is the lead certified Silva Method trainer for Russian-speaking markets, directing the Pozitum centre since 2009. She is one of very few certified Russian-language trainers in Europe and has trained students across Russia, Estonia, Germany and internationally.

Module 1 — Silva Life System (2 days)

Foundation: entering the alpha state, the glass of water technique, mental screen visualisation, stress and sleep management, goal-setting through mental rehearsal. Suitable for complete beginners.

Module 2 — Silva Intuition System (2 days)

Advanced: developing intuition, psychometry, case working, remote sensing. Builds directly on Module 1 skills. Recommended to complete both in sequence.

Summary: Which model works best?

For a 1-module event at 20–25 participants: both models work — Model A gives the organiser a slightly higher return; Model B is symmetric and may be easier to agree on. For a 2-module bundle event: Model A outperforms significantly at 22–25 participants (organiser €4,660–€6,400 vs Model B €3,930–€4,800 each). Revenue assumptions use 80% early bird + 20% normal pricing. For a first edition targeting 20+ participants: 1 module + blended €360 avg is the lowest-risk entry point.

Best Dates for 2026 — Saturday–Sunday–Monday–Tuesday Format

Module 1: Saturday–Sunday. Module 2: Monday–Tuesday. Attendees need to take 2 working days off — factor this into audience targeting (self-employed, flexible workers, people between jobs). Avoid Dutch school holidays and bank holidays.
