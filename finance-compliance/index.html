Key Finance & Compliance Trends — 2026
Counterfeiting
Global trade in counterfeits reached $467 billion — 2.3% of world imports
The OECD (2025) estimates counterfeit and pirated goods at $467B (2021 data). The EU loses €60B annually across 13 sectors (EUIPO). Clothing, pharma, and electronics are hardest hit.
Brand Protection
E-commerce platforms have become a primary distribution channel for fakes
The OECD (2021) documents how e-commerce platforms provide powerful storefronts for counterfeiters. Brand protection has shifted from customs enforcement to digital takedown operations — requiring new capabilities, data, and platform partnerships.
IP Enforcement
EU customs seized a record 152 million counterfeit items in 2023
EU Customs Report 2023: 152M items worth €3.4B seized — a 77% increase over 2022. China accounts for 56% of seizures, followed by Hong Kong (~9%) and Turkey (~8%).
Regulation
EU General Product Safety Regulation reshaping brand protection obligations
The GPSR (Regulation 2023/988), effective December 2024, extends obligations for product safety to online marketplaces. Brands and enforcement organisations now have new leverage to compel platforms to act on counterfeit listings.
Technology
Digital authentication methods are evolving anti-counterfeiting enforcement
Image recognition, NFC authentication, and serialisation technologies complement traditional enforcement. The EU Falsified Medicines Directive already mandates 2D barcode serialisation on all prescription medicines since February 2019.
NGO & Industry
Anti-counterfeiting organisations coordinate enforcement across 130+ countries
Not-for-profit organisations bridge brand owners and enforcement authorities. React (founded 1991, Amsterdam) operates in 130+ countries. ACG represents 3,000+ brands from the UK. INTA has 6,600+ member organisations across 182 countries (INTA, 2026).
