Key Real Estate Signals — 2026
Strong Growth
Romania, Hungary, Bulgaria: CEE remains the strongest growth story in Europe
Hungary leads the EU at +21% YoY (Eurostat HPI). Bulgaria adopted the euro Jan 1 2026, removing currency risk. Romania remains undervalued by ECB affordability metrics. CEE catch-up cycle continues.
Watch
Portugal and Greece showing affordability stress — growth may be peaking
Portugal's P2I ratio hit 153 (OECD average: 114.8). Both markets still growing (+17% Portugal, +7.7% Greece in 2025) but affordability is increasingly stretched.
Macro
ECB deposit rate at 2.0% — the rate-cut tailwind is largely priced in
Eight rate cuts from June 2024 brought the ECB deposit rate from 4.0% to 2.0%, sparking the 2025 recovery across Europe. With rates now stable, further price gains must come from fundamentals — not financing tailwinds.
Supply
Structural EU housing shortfall keeps long-term floor under prices
The EU needs 2+ million new homes per year through 2035 (EU JRC) but builds ~1.6 million. Germany completed only 251,900 in 2024 — down 14.4%, far below its 400,000 target.
Recovery
Germany and France: first signs of recovery after sharp corrections
Germany's −1.6% in 2024 reversed to +3.2% in 2025 — first growth after a two-year correction. France (−3.7% in 2024) is showing early stabilisation at ~+1%. Both markets are driven by ECB rate relief feeding through to mortgage costs.
Structural
Spain: strongest housing cycle in 18 years, driven by chronic supply shortage
Spain grew +11.2% in 2024 and +12–13% in 2025 — the strongest cycle since 2007. Foreign buyer demand from UK, Germany, Netherlands and Latin America is competing with near-zero supply of new homes at accessible price points.
