Brand Collaboration
Swatch closed 3 stores in the Netherlands on Saturday morning. A €385 watch did this.

The Swatch × Audemars Piguet Royal Pop launched on 16 May at selected boutiques worldwide. In Amsterdam, Leidschendam, and Utrecht, crowds grew unmanageable before 10:00 and three stores cancelled the launch. MoonSwatch precedent: a €260 Omega collaboration boosted original Speedmaster sales by 50%. The Royal Pop sells the DNA of a €20,000 watch at €385 — a 98% discount on brand equity, not the product.

Humanitarian Logistics
DHL has responded to 77 crises, moved 70,000+ tonnes of relief cargo, and gets logistics moving again in 72 hours.

When a crisis hits, the local supply chain collapses — airports stop functioning, cargo piles up, aid cannot move. DHL GoHelp has spent 20 years training 1,000+ volunteers to restore cargo operations in crisis zones within 72 hours, across 60+ airports in 30 countries.

Humanitarian Logistics
WFP, UNHCR and IOM cut nearly 19,000 jobs in 2025 — while 343 million people faced acute food insecurity.

WFP eliminated 7,000 positions (30% of workforce). UNHCR cut 5,900 (30%). IOM reduced headcount by 6,000 (35%). The cuts represent a contraction in the world's most complex logistics networks, driven by funding shortfalls after the US reduced its share of WFP's budget from 46%.

Behind every humanitarian mission: 8 European cities.

Social Commerce
Instagram reintroduced its affiliate program in March 2026 — 3.5 years after Meta shut it down.

Meta discontinued Instagram's affiliate program in August 2022. During the gap, TikTok Shop generated $5.1B in US GMV with creator commissions and YouTube Shopping enrolled 500,000+ creators with 5x YoY GMV growth. The March 2026 relaunch allows creators to tag any affiliate URL directly in Reels. The question is whether creators who built affiliate businesses elsewhere will return.

P.Louise (UK cosmetics) is generating €4.41M monthly revenue on TikTok Shop. Germany at €618K/month, France €400K/month, Spain €305K/month, Italy €179K/month. October 2025 figures — social commerce revenue is finally materialising at scale across European markets.

Social Commerce
Social commerce reached $908B in 2026. TikTok Shop is the only closed-loop checkout left in the West.

TikTok Shop: $15.1B US GMV in 2025, 68% YoY growth, projected 24.1% of US social commerce by 2027. Meta phased out native checkout by September 2025 after six years. Pinterest has 553M monthly users but has not had native checkout since 2018. YouTube Shopping is growing at 5x YoY GMV but does not publish figures.

70% of luxury consumers spent £1,000+ on a single item. No major luxury house has a TikTok Shop storefront.

TikTok Marketing Science/AYTM study (May 2025), 3,000+ luxury consumers across the UK, US, France and Italy. 38% discover luxury through user-generated content, 32% through creator videos, 15% bought a luxury item directly after seeing it on TikTok. Friday AI Digest #3
Companies are replacing entire teams with AI. The risk is removing the people who could tell the difference between a mistake and the truth.

When the humans leave, the benchmark leaves with them. AI errors don't announce themselves — they surface as normal output until someone with enough experience recognises something is off. Remove that person and the glitch becomes the new standard, silently compounding until the cost becomes visible.

Global EV Adoption
Norway leads global EV adoption at 92% market share, China sits at 48%, and the US is still at 10%.

Tesla Model Y is the top-selling vehicle overall in Norway. BYD outsells every Western competitor inside China. The US has fragmented state incentives and no dominant domestic player. Europe occupies the middle band, with VW, BMW and Volvo gaining ground against Tesla and BYD. Source: IEA Global EV Outlook 2025.

VW won Europe. Tesla is fourth.

VW Group leads Europe's battery-electric market in 2025 with 27% share, with the VW brand growing BEV sales 56%. Tesla dropped to 9% on a 26.9% YoY decline (from 326,525 to 238,656 units). BYD surged 268.6% to 7%. Stellantis and BMW Group both gained share and now sit ahead of Tesla.

EU 2030 Targets
The EU is one percentage point from its 2030 tertiary attainment target — and Ireland is already at 65.2%.

EU average tertiary attainment for 25–34 year-olds: 44.1% in 2025, against the 45% target for 2030. Ireland leads the EU at 65.2%, Luxembourg at 63.8%, Lithuania at 58.2%. Germany sits at 39.9% with its vocational tradition. Italy at 31.6% and Romania at 23.2% mark the central EU-27 gap. 13 member states are above target, 14 below.

Future of Work
Pay $347,000 to Harvard. Or under $300 to Google. To get the same job.

EU public university tuition averages €7,800. An edX MicroMasters costs €600–€2,000. Deloitte, Verizon, Target and others now formally recognise Google certificates. By 2030, what will employers actually value: signal or skills?
