The European tuition gap is widening. Post-Brexit, EU students now pay international rates at UK universities — making Dutch, Belgian, and German universities far more competitive on price. The Netherlands offers world-class English-taught bachelor programs at €2,209–€2,530/year, including TU Delft (#47 QS), Wageningen (#57), and the University of Amsterdam (#100). For chemistry specifically, the Netherlands has strong English-medium options alongside a full Dutch-language landscape.

Countries with Free (or Near-Free) Bachelor Education — EU Students
For EU/EEA students, these countries offer the most affordable higher education in Europe
🇩🇪
Germany
~€300/year
Public universities charge only a Semesterbeitrag (admin fee, ~€150–350/semester) — not tuition. This applies to EU and non-EU students alike at most states. Catch: most bachelor programs are in German; English-taught bachelors are limited but growing (especially TUM, Jacobs University).
🇩🇰
Denmark
Free
EU/EEA students pay no tuition at Danish public universities (University of Copenhagen, Aarhus, DTU, SDU). Some bachelor programs taught in Danish; English availability is growing especially at DTU and international programs. Non-EU students pay ~DKK 45,000–120,000/year.
🇸🇪
Sweden
Free
EU/EEA students pay no tuition at Swedish public universities (Chalmers, KTH, Stockholm, Uppsala, Lund, Gothenburg). Most bachelor programs are in Swedish; English is more common at master's level. Non-EU students pay ~SEK 80,000–145,000/year.
🇫🇮
Finland
Free
EU/EEA students pay no tuition at Finnish universities (Helsinki, Aalto, Tampere). English bachelor programs are limited — most are in Finnish or Swedish. Exception: Aalto has strong English-medium engineering and business programs. Non-EU = fees apply.
🇳🇴
Norway
Free for everyone
Norway's public universities are free for all nationalities — including non-EU. NTNU, University of Oslo, Bergen. English programs available but limited at bachelor level. Students need to cover living costs (~NOK 12,000–15,000/month).
🇦🇹
Austria
~€363/semester (~€726/yr)
Public universities charge a nominal tuition fee for EU/EEA students. University of Vienna, TU Vienna, Graz are accessible and internationally respected. Most programs are in German at bachelor level; English is limited.
🇨🇿
Czech Republic
Free in Czech language
Czech-language programs at public universities are free for all. Charles University (Prague, #355 QS) and Brno (Masaryk) have strong reputations. English-taught programs cost €2,000–8,000/year. Best value if you speak Czech or plan to learn.
🇧🇪
Belgium
~€960/year
Flemish public universities (KU Leuven, Ghent, VUB) charge low statutory fees for EU students (~€960–1,100/year). KU Leuven is ranked #67 globally and offers international programs. Many bachelor programs are in Dutch; English options exist in sciences and engineering.
Chemistry & Chemistry-Related Bachelor Programs in English — Europe
Focused on programs with significant chemistry content, taught in English, open to EU/international students
Imperial College London
🇬🇧 United Kingdom · QS #2
BSc Chemistry / BSc Chemistry with Medicinal Chemistry
World-leading chemistry department. Strong research integration from year 1. MChem option (4 years). Specialist tracks in computational chemistry, green chemistry, materials.
£35,100/yr (EU = intl rate)
UCL
🇬🇧 United Kingdom · QS #9
BSc Chemistry / BSc Chemistry with a Year Abroad
Strong physical and organic chemistry. Year abroad option (Netherlands, Germany, France). Excellent lab facilities in central London. MSci route available.
~£29,000/yr (EU = intl rate)
University of Edinburgh
🇬🇧 United Kingdom · QS #27
BSc / MChem Chemistry
4-year integrated Scottish degree (MChem). Strong organic, biological, and computational chemistry. Year in industry option. Top Russell Group chemistry.
~£26,600/yr (EU = intl rate)
ETH Zurich
🇨🇭 Switzerland · QS #7
BSc Chemistry (taught in German at BSc level)
World #1 for chemistry in some rankings. BSc is in German; English teaching starts at MSc. Strong materials and computational chemistry. Very competitive entry. Master's fully in English.
CHF 1,460/yr (~€1,600)
University of Amsterdam (UvA)
🇳🇱 Netherlands · QS #100
BSc Chemistry (English track available)
Full chemistry bachelor with strong analytical, organic, and physical tracks. Amsterdam Science Park campus with direct links to research institutes (AMOLF, CWI). Both Dutch and English streams.
€2,314/yr
Leiden University + TU Delft
🇳🇱 Netherlands · QS Leiden #163 / Delft #47
BSc Life Science & Technology (English, joint program)
Unique joint bachelor combining Leiden's natural sciences with TU Delft's engineering. Strong chemistry, biology, and biotechnology foundation. Fully in English. Excellent for students interested in chemistry + engineering.
€2,209/yr
Trinity College Dublin
🇮🇪 Ireland · QS #82
BSc Science — Chemistry stream
Broad science entry with chemistry specialisation from year 2. Strong analytical and medicinal chemistry tradition. English-medium. Good industry links (pharma sector in Ireland).
€3,000/yr (EU student contribution)
KU Leuven
🇧🇪 Belgium · QS #67
BSc Chemistry (Dutch/Flemish; English tracks in sciences)
Exceptional chemistry research university. Most bachelor programs are in Dutch (Flemish); English entry possible via international bachelor programs or direct transfer. Strong pharmaceutical and materials chemistry.
€960/yr
University of Copenhagen
🇩🇰 Denmark · QS #121
BSc Chemistry (Danish) / BSc Biochemistry
Strong Scandinavian chemistry tradition. Most BSc programs in Danish; English instruction grows at year 3. International students often study a preparatory Danish language semester. Free for EU students.
€0 for EU/EEA
TU/e Eindhoven
🇳🇱 Netherlands · QS #155
BSc Chemical Engineering and Chemistry (Dutch)
Top technical university for chemical engineering and materials science. BSc is taught in Dutch; English enters from year 2+. Strong industry partnerships (ASML, DSM/Firmenich). International BSc track being expanded.
€2,209/yr
Wageningen University
🇳🇱 Netherlands · QS #57
BSc Molecular Life Sciences (English)
Chemistry-heavy life sciences bachelor, fully in English. Strong in biochemistry, molecular biology, food chemistry, and environmental chemistry. Top-ranked globally for agriculture and life sciences.
€2,209/yr
Radboud University
🇳🇱 Netherlands · QS ~#250
BSc Molecular Science (English)
Interdisciplinary program covering chemistry, physics, and biology at the molecular level. Taught fully in English. Located in Nijmegen. Strong research integration with Radboud University Medical Center and IMM.
€2,314/yr

Chemistry-Related Programs Worth Considering (Netherlands)
BSc Farmaceutische Wetenschappen — Utrecht, Amsterdam, Groningen. Strong analytical chemistry content within pharmaceutical sciences.
BSc Nanobiology — TU Delft + Erasmus MC (Rotterdam). Joint program: chemistry, biology, physics at the nanoscale. English-taught.
BSc Biomedical Sciences — Multiple NL universities, many in English. Biology + chemistry + medical focus. Excellent for future pharma/biotech careers.
BSc Liberal Arts & Sciences (UCU) — Utrecht University College. English, residential. Allows chemistry + other science combination.
