Trend Intelligence

Digital Identity
Every EU Member State must offer a Digital Identity Wallet by late 2026
Under Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 (eIDAS 2.0), which entered into force on 20 May 2024, each Member State must make at least one EU Digital Identity Wallet available to citizens within 24 months. Where online public services require electronic identification, they must accept the wallet. Source: European Commission — eIDAS 2.0
AI Regulation
The EU AI Act becomes fully applicable on 2 August 2026 — including for public authorities
Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 entered into force on 1 August 2024. Prohibited AI practices have applied since 2 February 2025; general-purpose AI model rules since 2 August 2025. High-risk AI systems used by public authorities placed on the market before August 2026 have a transitional compliance deadline of 2 August 2030. Source: European Commission — AI Act
Digital Decade
80% of citizens to use a digital ID solution by 2030 — a key 2030 public-service target
The EU Digital Decade Policy Programme sets measurable 2030 targets for public-sector digitalisation, including 100% online availability of key public services for citizens and businesses and 80% of citizens using a digital ID solution. Source: European Commission — Digital Decade
Government Benchmarking
The eGovernment Benchmark tracks how digital EU public services actually are
The annual eGovernment Benchmark assesses 37 countries on user-centricity, transparency, key enablers and cross-border mobility. The 2024 edition shows EU27+ steady progress but persistent gaps in cross-border service availability. Source: EC — eGovernment Benchmark 2024
Higher Education
Erasmus+ 2021-2027 programme has a €26.2 billion budget for EU education mobility
The Erasmus+ 2021-2027 programme, the EU's flagship for education, training, youth and sport, has a budget of €26.2 billion — nearly double the 2014-2020 envelope. It funds learning mobility for students, staff, trainees and teachers across the Union. Source: Erasmus+
European Universities
65 European Universities alliances now connect 570+ higher-education institutions
The European Universities Initiative supports transnational alliances of higher-education institutions across Europe. As of 2024 the programme spans 65 alliances involving more than 570 higher-education institutions across EU Member States and associated countries. Source: EC — European Universities Initiative
